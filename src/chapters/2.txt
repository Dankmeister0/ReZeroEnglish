Arc 1 Part 1: "The Serrated Ten-Yen Coin is Useless"

—This has turned into a seriously bad situation.

As he stood at a loss, completely broke, his mind was filled with that single thought.
To say he was "penniless" wasn't strictly accurate. His wallet was in his pocket, and aside from the fact that it had a bit too much change and too few bills, it contained his entire net worth.
He had enough on him to go to the nearest shopping mall, buy something at a bookstore, and have some lunch. Despite that, he had no choice but to describe himself as penniless.
After all,

"As I thought, the currency value is completely different..."

Flicking the ten-yen coin in his hand—a rare "serrated edge" one—the boy let out a long sigh.
He was a boy with no particularly distinguishing features. Short black hair, of an average height that was neither tall nor short. His build was somewhat muscular, perhaps from training, which combined with his cheap gray tracksuit to give him the air of a sportsman.
Only his sharp, sanpaku eyes left an impression, but now even the corners of those eyes drooped listlessly, devoid of any spirit.

He looked so ordinary that he would likely be lost in a crowd in an instant.
However, the gazes of the people looking at him were thick with an inexplicable hue, as if they were looking at something "bizarre."

It was a natural reaction—after all, among those watching the boy, not a single one had black hair or wore a tracksuit.
Their hair colors ranged from blonde, white, and brown to green and even blue. Furthermore, their attire consisted of armor, dancer-like outfits, or pitch-black robes, looking far too "authentic."

Exposed to the wave of unscrupulous stares, the boy could only fold his arms and accept the reality.

"In other words, this is that."

He snapped his fingers and pointed them at the people watching him.

"—It seems I've been summoned to another world."

In front of him, a carriage-like vehicle pulled by a giant lizard-like creature crossed the path.

Subaru Natsuki was born in Heisei-era Japan and was a product of the "relaxed education" generation.

His life had spanned seventeen years, and to tell the full story of it would require exactly seventeen years.
Omitting all that and explaining his current position simply: he was a "third-year high school shut-in."
To be more detailed, he was "the kind of hopeless scum who, despite being close to entrance exams, threw away his parents' expectations and everything else to lock himself in his own shell."

There was no particular reason for his withdrawal.
It started on an ordinary weekday when he happened to think, "Waking up today is a pain," and decided to skip.
From there, his self-imposed school absences gradually increased, and before he knew it, he was a full-fledged shut-in making his parents cry.
Spending his days in idleness, constantly submerged in the communication-free internet—

"And the result is being summoned to another world... Even saying it myself, it makes no sense."

Reconfirming his situation, Subaru let out his umpteenth sigh.

Moving away from the street where he had been receiving curious stares, he was now sitting in a slightly dim back alley.
The ground was paved; while the work was crude compared to modern Japan, it wasn't bad.

"Assuming the current situation is an isekai fantasy, is the civilization the standard medieval style? From what I've seen, there's no machinery, and the building materials are mostly unified as stone or wood..."

Recalling the sights he saw before sitting down in the alley, he organized the information in his head.
Thanks to having plenty of time for daydreams on a daily basis, his mental preparation for being "summoned to another world" was top-notch.

First, he needed to calmly assess the civilization of the era and the differences in food, clothing, and shelter compared to modern Japan. He also had to determine differences in physical phenomena and whether contact with the "humanoid creatures" living here was possible.

"Alright, good, me. I didn't daydream for nothing. Confirmation of civilization level is mostly done; for now, money won't work. Plus, I was able to talk to a shopkeeper, so communication isn't an issue."

Upon realizing he had been summoned, the first thing Subaru did was attempt to negotiate with a "greengrocer?" He tried to buy an "apple?" displayed at the storefront, but his Japanese yen was rejected.
From what he saw then, the currency in this world seemed to be gold, silver, and copper coins. The ease of understanding a worldview where the currency itself holds value was very typical of an isekai fantasy.

"Well, they'll probably decline because of mixtures or inferior products, like when 500-won coins showed up."

*They'd be too heavy to carry around anyway,* he muttered to himself as another lizard-drawn carriage passed the street. Dust billowed up grandly, but the people passing by were either used to it or indifferent.

"Still, there are fewer of them compared to cars. ...Come to think of it, I haven't seen any dogs or cats yet."

The giant lizard pulling the "carriage?" was perhaps one size larger than a horse. Since it was lean, its overall mass didn't seem much different, but the sight of a reptile that large felt incredibly wrong.

"It's probably... common. Both the lizards and the way people look."

And the part he had left for last was the peculiar appearance of the humans in this world.

He accepted that hair colors were colorful. Theoretically, you could have any hair color if you dyed it, and since this was an isekai fantasy, he had already come to terms with that.
The part he considered a problem was something else—for example, "beast ears."

From a quick glance, he had spotted "dog ears" and "cat ears." There were "bunnies," and for more unusual ones, he thought he saw something like a "lizardman" for a moment.
On the other hand, there were people who looked no different from Subaru, so the conclusion was—

"The genre is isekai fantasy. The civilization is typical medieval style. Demi-humans are common, and there are probably wars and adventures. Animals are slightly different, but their roles haven't changed—I guess."

After organizing that much, Subaru let out a long breath that wasn't quite a sigh.

Putting his situation into words, he frowned at the opportunistic nature of the development.
If things went according to his daydreamed scenarios, he should be using his modern knowledge to play the "I'm OP" role from here on out, but the worldview was subtly different from one where his prepared knowledge could be used.

"My simulation for being sent back to the Sengoku period was perfect, though... If I'd been sent to the Sengoku period, I would've helped Nobunaga take over the world."

In an isekai fantasy, the knowledge he could use was at most the ratio for mixing black powder.
Even that might be meaningless depending on the civilization level of this world. If "magic," a staple of isekai fantasies, existed, then gunpowder would probably be treated as nothing more than fireworks.

"Well, it's also a kind of trope that magic isn't omnipotent. If there's a chance to contribute to the development of science or something, I'll do my best... but the immediate problem is..."

The fact was that he had no idea why he was summoned, or for what purpose.

He remembered clearly what happened before he was summoned. He had left his house for the first time in a while and was on his way back from buying cup ramen for a late-night snack at a convenience store. He wasn't in the mood for his bike, so he went on foot.
And on the way, he remembered looking up at the night sky and thinking, "Tonight is one step away from a full moon."
Then, when he looked down and blinked, it was suddenly daytime.

From night to day in an instant.
He knew immediately that an abnormal situation had occurred. But that was all he knew.
He was calm now, but his panic immediately following the event was a display of cowardice that should be passed down through generations.

"Well, if I, the eldest son, am missing, there won't be any future generations to tell anyway."

Muttering this, Subaru once again checked his belongings.
In an isekai fantasy situation, initial equipment was more important than he had imagined. Right now, the fact that he was connected by even the thinnest thread was important.

First, his cell phone (battery about to die), his wallet (many video rental store membership cards), the cup ramen he bought at the convenience store (tonkotsu soy sauce flavor), snack chips (corn pottage flavor), his favorite gray tracksuit (unwashed), and his worn-out sneakers (two years old). That was it.

"I'm finished... Why don't I have even a single pistol? What am I supposed to do?"

The only thing that might be useful was the snack to fill his stomach. Once it eased his hunger, that would be the end of it.

"The situation is hopeless. And as expected, the cause is unknown. I don't remember going through a mirror or falling into a pond, and above all, if this is a summoning, where's the beautiful girl who summoned me?"

In a sense, the main heroine was absent. From the perspective of the 2D world, this was an unthinkable neglect of duty.
To be summoned and then left aimlessly was like being used and tossed away.
In fact, having finished assessing his current situation, Subaru could no longer even escape reality and could only hang his head.

"Seriously, give me a break. What do you want me to do?"

Weak words and complaints leaked out; he was already on the verge of breaking. He just wanted to go home.
Daydreams were fine staying as daydreams. Summoning to another world was fun when you could be peerless in your head and act like you're OP; if you're actually thrown into one, what else can you do but shrink back?

"Anyway, for the time being, the goal is to survive... but can I make it with my 'Communication Level 1'?"

Aside from his family, the only people he had properly conversed with were convenience store clerks. He had lived that kind of life for nearly a year. He had long since forgotten how to gauge the distance between people.

"In a chat, I can type at the same speed as I talk..."

Subaru felt anxious about the future while wiggling his fingers.
Then, his expression changed. The reason was a sound.
Footsteps suddenly echoed in the back alley—looking toward the entrance of the alley, three men stood there, blocking the path.
