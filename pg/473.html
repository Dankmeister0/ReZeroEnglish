"—Hey, do you know my name?"

At that question, the girl, Amyu Sears, caught her breath slightly.

It was an ordinary, trivial question. It should have been a boring question that required neither much time nor resolve to answer. If you know it, you know it. If you don't, you don't. That should have been all.

"————"

However, at a question that seemed so simple on paper, Amyu could not find her voice.

Life is a series of choices. That was a truth that even Amyu, who had only just turned fourteen, had learned well enough through the experiences of her short life.

In life, one must make decisions about everything. It starts with the trivialities of daily life and ranges to major decisions that sway the course of one's existence. But regardless of the scale, life is made entirely of decisions.

And now, the question posed to Amyu Sears carried the greatest weight of her fourteen years, and perhaps represented the biggest decision of her life. And yet, it was that very same boring, unspecial question.

"—Hey, do you know my name?"

The repeated question tightened around Amyu's throat. However, the person who posed the question seemingly had no such intention. Repeating the question once more was an act of kindness; the sense of consideration felt strangely unbalanced.

If the one tormenting Amyu was the questioner, the one most concerned for Amyu was also the questioner. She understood that all he truly sought was the answer to the question. That was why Amyu had no choice but to grope for the correct option without any clues, finding it through a dialogue with her inner self.

—What, exactly, was the correct answer?

Did knowing it or not knowing it better suit the questioner's expectations? Or, even if she didn't know, would it be better to say she did? Even if she did know, would it be better to say she didn't? —Amyu's heart screamed at the restricted choice.

"—Hey, do you... know... my... name?"

As if growing impatient, the phrasing of the repeated question was broken down into finer segments. Perhaps he was worried that his words weren't getting through; that anxiety clawed at Amyu's chest. Honestly, she was certain that failing to say anything, failing to produce an answer, and failing to make a decision was the one thing that would absolutely never suit the questioner's intent.

She couldn't end this without saying anything, without giving an answer, without making a decision. She wouldn't be released. —That much was certain.

"————"

Unable to speak, Amyu stared back into the black pupils directly in front of her and pondered. Inside those black pupils that looked empty, she saw her own haggard, terrible face.

Though she didn't go out of her way to say it, she had been aware that her appearance was decent enough to be looked at, but now there was no trace of that; she was simply cowering, terrified by the dominant power of the questioner before her. As if she had aged decades in an instant, Amyu's face was exhausted. She felt so weak, as if she might die from the pressure of the question alone—,

"—Hey, do you know my name?"

Suddenly, the pressure that suggested death instead gave Amyu a sense of hope. While experiencing the sensation of her chest being crushed by breathlessness, a premonition took hold of her: that by answering the question, she might be released from it. The moment she thought that, she felt the heavy sensation dominating her entire body begin to fade.

The breathlessness came from the pressure of suspicion and doubt over what she should answer. Because she wanted to be released from that, she suppressed her anxiety regarding the initial question. It might have been a kind of self-preservation instinct, but to Amyu in this moment, it felt like a divine revelation. Therefore, her lips trembled, and she stared back into the black pupils once more.

"—Hey, do you know my name?"

By answering the question, she would be released from this moment's breathlessness. With that single-minded thought, Amyu finally moved her twitching tongue and spun the words.

"N-No..."

She didn't know; Amyu followed her heart and answered so. Moving her thoughts away from the choices that had been spinning endlessly in her head, she answered the question with a pure state of mind.

In fact, she knew neither the name nor the face of the questioner before her. Living in a remote backwater, a place that should be called the frontier, even the great events of the kingdom she heard rumors of felt like occurrences in a far-off land. So, no matter how great a person he might be, to her, he was a stranger—,

"—I see."

It was a short sentence. She couldn't distinguish whether the emotion contained in those words was deep feeling or disappointment. However, Amyu's decision had been made. The rest was a matter for the questioner.

If life is a series of choices and decisions, then it is the same for everyone. Just as Amyu had chosen, the questioner would also choose.

"————"

Quietly, the fourteen-year-old girl waited for the questioner's choice.

—Alone in the middle of a village where no one but herself remained, she waited for the answer.
