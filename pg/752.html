Arc 9, Part 56: "Petra Leyte"

—Petra Leyte was a country girl born in Arlam Village, a small settlement within the Kingdom of Lugunica.

Though blessed with extraordinary looks and innate cleverness, she possessed nothing else particularly noteworthy. Under normal circumstances, she would have had no place in events that would be etched into history; she likely would have lived out her life as an ordinary villager, perhaps forever dreaming of the city.

Therefore, the current state of Petra—having strayed from her "original" path and becoming involved in numerous world-shaking incidents—was something distorted by outside interference.

Petra herself was aware that her destiny had been warped.

"Originally," Petra had been a somewhat cringeworthy girl who was overconfident in her own cuteness, mistakenly believing she could wrap both adults and children around her little finger. Surely, the "original" Petra would have spent her days acting as the self-indulgent ruler of her own narrow world, never having that delusion corrected.

She didn't necessarily think that would have been a bad life. She imagined it would have been fun and happy in its own way.

It was just that the current Petra, who had strayed from that "original" path, could no longer view such a thing as happiness.

"Because, in that world, none of the people I love would be there, right?"

Subaru wouldn't be there. Frederica wouldn't be there. Emilia, Beatrice, Meili, Ram, Rem, Ryuzu, Annerose, Garfiel, Otto, and Clind wouldn't be there. Roswaal... well, he could wait. But everyone else would be gone.

"The very thought of it gives me the creeps."

If the "original" her was someone who never met the people she loved most, and the her standing here was the result of some collision of fates, then she was truly glad she had strayed.

As a result of taking a path that wasn't "original," she had encountered one painful, agonizing, sad, and bitter experience after another—situations where she nearly died, or situations where dying seemed preferable.

But more than those hardships, there were things she had been given, things she had grasped.

That was why Petra Leyte could say from the bottom of her heart:

"Even if I could redo my life many times over, I would stray from the 'original' path again."

And she would want to meet him again and again, to tell him this.

Because that person, who knew the same suffering as her, looked toward the stars with such an expression of pain and sorrow.

Petra Leyte—

—God, Buddha, Od Laguna. I swear that for the rest of my life, I will never see anyone off on a journey.

"Ngh—!"

Exhaling sharply, she kicked off the hard, rocky ground and ran recklessly.

Correcting her form, leaning forward—she made a start so ideal she could have fallen in love with it herself. She ran faster than she ever had in her life; she ran, and ran, and ran.

The sound of blood rushing through her body felt deafening; her heart hammered against her ribs as if it were about to explode; she left her own ragged breath behind as she ran.

Even after running that hard—

"—Play ball."

The moment that cold declaration reached her back, her vision shifted completely.

Before she knew it, the legs that had been running faster than ever before had stopped, and she was standing dazed on the rocky ground. Before her eyes was the familiar sight she was supposed to have turned her back on. Within it, the man in the iron helmet muttered:

"—Territory Expansion: Matrix Redefinition."

This moment always began with that declaration. And within her field of vision, her comrades—unaware of the man's scheme—raised their voices in a roar that shook the atmosphere.

"Let's do this, you guys!!" ""—OHH!!""

Centered around the brave girl's shout, the group's morale reached its peak and exploded. Driven by that momentum, they lunged at the iron-helmeted man all at once. —But it was no use.

The result was already decided. No matter what they did, their outstretched hands would never reach him.

"————"

As if their strings had been cut, thirty-seven people collapsed one by one.

It looked as if they fell simultaneously. However, she already knew from closely observing each individual's fall that there was a subtle order to it. Though, the difference between the first person falling and the thirty-seventh was barely two seconds.

There was no room to intervene in those few seconds, and the challenge ended in vain.

A voice reached her eardrums with that dry sentiment—no, not her eardrums. This voice was different from one that vibrated through the air, so it wasn't her eardrums that shook.

If it wasn't her eardrums, then what was it that shook?

It's obvious. It's your heart.

Her heart. —Yes, that was it. It was because her heart shook that she could hear it.

There was still room for her heart to shake. As proof of that, he was there.

And his name was—

"—Subaru."

Yeah, I'm right here, Petra.

When his lips called her "Petra," she felt a wave of relief.

That's right. She was Petra Leyte. She still held onto that identity without letting go.

If that was the case, then, therefore, if so, then—

"—Play ball."

—Petra Leyte had to continue this journey through hell.

—God, Buddha, Od Laguna. I swear that for the rest of my life, I will never receive charity from anyone.

"—Territory Expansion: Matrix Redefinition." "—Play ball."

A moment that began with that declaration and ended with the other.

How much time had passed while Petra was trapped in this loop that lasted less than a minute?

"...Wait, not even a single second has passed."

Retorting to the whisper in her own heart, Petra let out a raspy "Ha."

She thought to herself that it was a terribly cynical, self-deprecating, and world-weary mutter. She was surprised that she was even capable of making a voice so clouded with dark emotions.

But it wasn't a mistake, nor was it a mishearing of someone else's voice.

Because here—

"There's only my voice, those people's voices, that man's voice, and Subaru's."
