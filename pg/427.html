Re:Zero EX: "Accreting Life in Another World from Zero (Kasaneru/Greed IF)"

This is the annual April Fools' project! This is not the main story, but a story of an IF world! The content is a branch from Arc 4, Chapter 74 of "Re:Zero -Starting Life in Another World-," so please be careful if you have not read that far yet.

Furthermore, since this is a story of a world completely different from the main story, the relationships between characters have changed significantly. Please understand this. Only those who can enjoy it with that understanding, please proceed.

※　※　※　※　※　※　※　※　※　※　※　※　※

A dream—he was having a dream.

A dream that did not fade, a dream that repeated, an endless dream, a dream he would not let end—.

Accumulating time and again, redoing time and again, erring time and again, correcting time and again. Spinning a thousand times. Connecting ten thousand times. Surpassing a hundred million times. Before he knew it, he had forgotten how to count.

There is pain, there is shock, there is confusion, there is ruin, there is hatred, and there is madness. He is worn down, shaken, twisted, decayed, made to wail, and his heart is shattered.

Even so, there is a place he wants to reach. Even so, there is a wish he wants to protect.

Even if no one knows the shape of the tragedy that is repeated and accumulated, so that he does not forget. Even if no one ever notices, so that he alone can never forget.

—Even if the person he wants to save cries, he wants to save them.

That is why he has no regrets, even now, for taking that hand. If there was anything to regret, it was his own weak, fragile heart that failed to reach the strength of steel, feeling hesitation and doubt in taking that hand.

—When he awakens from a hollow dream, the first thing he always feels is a dull headache.

"————"

Opening his eyes while resisting the fingertips of drowsiness, he blinks a few times to bring his consciousness to the surface. Vaguely, an indistinct mist remains over his consciousness, but the poor blood circulation lasts only a few seconds. Soon, his consciousness peels away from sleep, and awakening brings vitality to his body.

"Ah, aaah—"

Contrary to the awakening of his body, a drawn-out voice leaks from his mouth. At first glance, it might seem like the behavior of someone half-asleep, but this too is one of the important morning rituals. While lying on his back, by letting out his voice like this, various pieces of information flow in.

The tone of his voice, the establishment of self-awareness, the safety of his limbs, the organization of memories, the execution of his daily routine, the presence of life—. By performing fixed actions every morning without fail, these things are confirmed.

That itself serves as proof that Natsuki Subaru was able to greet the morning without any major issues.

"Fuaah."

With a yawn, he pushes aside the duvet and sits up by swinging down his raised legs. Scratching his head roughly, he looks around to see a familiar room—the interior of his own room, furnished with reasonably luxurious items and a canopy bed, comes into view. However, Subaru did not wake up in the canopy bed, but on a sofa placed in the back of the room. Wrapping himself in a blanket there and curling up to spend the night has been Natsuki Subaru's sleeping habit for these past—no, for these last few years.

That said, there isn't a particularly grand reason for not sleeping in the bed. He is simply struggling to provide even a slightly better sleeping environment. As a result of such trial and error, he learned that he feels a sense of relief sleeping on the sofa rather than the bed. He has done so ever since. That is all there is to it.

"————"

Rubbing his eyes, Subaru steps down from the sofa he uses as a bed and heads to the washroom to get ready. He washes his face in the washroom adjacent to the bedroom, reflects his wet face in the mirror, and observes his own face closely. A countenance where fatigue remains, and a gaze that suggests a sense of hollow exhaustion. Combined with his sloppily relaxed cheeks, he was missing all three marks and looked spiritless. To tighten that face, Subaru slaps his own cheeks with both hands as hard as he can. Tears well up from the dry sound and the numbing pain, and he splashes cold water on his face to wash them away. Then, reflecting his wet face in the mirror again, he carefully kneads his expression, gaze, and cheeks while praying.

"Smile, me. If you can't, die."

Chanting the magic spell, Subaru then forms a mean-looking smile with the corners of his mouth distorted. The smile, showing white teeth and narrowing his sanpaku eyes, is the villainous face he has been stuck with for over eighteen years. His eyes, expression, and complexion are all being 'mimicked' without issue.

"All right, good, keep it up."

Confirming that smile, Subaru wipes his face with a towel and quickly starts changing his clothes. When spending time in the mansion, Subaru's attire is not a servant's uniform—but rather, the reasonably prestigious formal wear of a nobleman. However, he has dressed it down by taking off the stiff jacket and rolling up the sleeves of his white shirt. But such attire, appropriate for the outside world, is now naturally required of him. It is all somewhat suffocating, but it was also unavoidable. Dressing appropriately for one's position is an obligation, and it is a position that Subaru himself desired and obtained.

"It's almost time for the usual."

Finishing his change of clothes, he looks up at the magic clock crystal installed above the door and mutters. The crystal, glowing a deep green, indicated the arrival of morning and that his scheduled wake-up time was imminent. It seems he spent a bit too much time forming his smile this morning. In a few minutes, a girl who is punctual with time will knock on the room door as scheduled. Before that, he must finish what needs to be done.

"————"

Switching his consciousness, Subaru bares the chest of his shirt. Hanging there is a pendant, which he never removes even while sleeping, connected by a thin chain to a 'black crystal.' Holding the mysteriously glittering black crystal in his palm, Subaru closed his eyes. The sensation of the cold, hard crystal stone—as soon as it was enveloped in Subaru's palm, its presence increased as if it had suddenly gained heat, and it began to pulse as if it were a living creature.

Naturally, his subconscious began to count the rhythm and frequency of the strange heartbeat generated in his palm. A crystal stone that continues to beat faster and higher than a pulse after a full sprint. That pulsation eventually overlapped with Subaru's own heartbeat, and the heart's thumping and the crystal stone's pulsing became equal. When his brain understood this, Subaru's consciousness was released from the yoke of his physical body and was invited into another space that could not exist in reality.

In an instant, sound and presence vanished from the world, and instead, a dazzling light enveloped his consciousness.

"————"

His consciousness, painted over by light, is slowly led to awakening. Opening his closed eyelids, what pours down is sunlight so bright it stings his eyes. And what spread out before his blinking eyes was a magnificent grassland where the wind blew through.

A vast, green grassland that continues forever. A sea of grass stretches as far as the eye can see, and the clusters of short grass sway softly in the gentle breeze. Overhead, there is a clear sky without a single cloud, and the pouring sunlight glittered like jewels. The blue of the sky and the green of the earth continue endlessly, and they meet faintly at the far-off horizon. There was a silence that felt eternal, fitting for a dreamlike world lacking a sense of reality.

A magnificent, clear, and empty space. —In such a world, there is only one heterogeneous part.

"————"

In the center of the grassland, if Subaru, standing there, looks back, there is a gentle slope right behind him, which leads to a small hill. On top of the hill is a modest flower garden, and right beside those lovely flowers, there was a parasol catching the sunlight and a white table.

"————"

In silence, Subaru climbs the hill and steps under the parasol. On the white table sits a steaming cup, and the ceramic was warmly filled with an amber liquid. Two cups were laid out, and white chairs were placed as if sandwiching the table. One was prepared for himself; without even confirming, Subaru sat in the chair, put his lips to the cup that seemed to have just been brewed, and moistened his throat with the tea.

—As usual, it's a strange tea that feels neither delicious nor disgusting.

However, he must always take the first sip of this tea. That was the promise he made with the master of this place, and it served as a greeting for their strange relationship. However—,

"Ah—, drank it, drank it, thanks for the treat. Now, let's get straight to the point."

"—It's true that I said the first sip is as good as a greeting. However, I don't believe that meant it's fine not to actually greet me."

Subaru's words after draining the tea and tossing the cup onto the table. In response to that content, the person facing him across the table filed a complaint while smiling faintly. To that grievance, Subaru scratched the bridge of his nose with his finger and said,

"...Is a greeting even necessary? For someone like you who observes the inside of my head twenty-four-seven, meeting or parting probably doesn't mean anything anyway."

"That's a separate matter. In the first place, I'd like you to stop using such a scandalous way of saying I'm peeking at you twenty-four-seven. Even if that were the case, I am at an age where I'm a maiden who puts flowers to shame. If it were known that I was constantly staring at the boy I'm interested in, it would be beyond scandalous."

"A maiden who puts flowers to shame? Did the concept of how to use words change while I wasn't looking?"

"How harsh. Well, it might be a bit of a stretch to insist on being a maiden at my age."

Saying so, the other person's mouth relaxed enjoyably, contrary to the content of the complaint. An exchange of banter they were completely used to; the other person was quite tolerant of Subaru's impolite attitude. —Or rather, it seemed as though she was even enjoying such verbal sparring.

Being smiled at like that, Subaru's heart couldn't help but feel a mounting, distorted sense of guilt. Even so, he didn't even know what the correct way to interact with her was. Only against the person in front of him did the means of finding that optimal solution fail completely.

"As I thought, you're a nasty woman."

"I'm glad to hear that. For someone as kind and greedy as you, rather than being one of the many likable people, or an indifferent stranger you find no value in reaching out to save, it's far more pleasing for me to be the hateful person who cruelly wounds your heart and drives in an unremovable wedge."

To someone so thoroughly seasoned, sarcasm and spite do not work. Subaru snorted with a displeased look at the response, but that only resulted in pleasing the other person even more.

"Now then, it would hurt my heart to buy any more of your resentment and be hated. It's about time we fulfill the purpose of this morning's tryst."

"Wasn't being hated what you wanted?"

"It's what you'd call a girl's cute bluff. I'd like you to see through it and accept it."

"Cute...?"

To Subaru, who tilted his head with genuine suspicion, the other person gave a faint smile. And then—,

"Truly, you are a human who is undaunted to the very end. As the 'Witch of Greed,' I find that part of you quite to my liking. —Natsuki Subaru."

That her contractor was worthy of her, the 'Witch of Greed'— Echidna narrowed her eyes enchantingly and smiled with genuine happiness.

"Today is the fourteenth day of the Month of Kisdam. ...There's no mistake about that, right?"

"You can rest assured. You fell asleep last night without incident and woke up this morning without incident. No worst-case scenario occurred during the night. You don't have to worry so much."

"Don't be stupid. There have been times when I went to sleep thinking nothing was wrong, only to find I'd died when I woke up. No matter how much I brace myself, it's never enough to know when or where I'll die."

Clicking his tongue at the Witch's optimism, Subaru recalls the 'Death' where he tasted a hopeless sense of loss. Just idly, the tomorrow he never doubted would surely come—how much terror and disappointment it brought when it was snatched away without warning, when he 'Returned by Death' out of the blue, and faced the reality of being forgotten by the people he had spent time with; there was no way he could forget.

"You're right. I was being careless just now. I apologize."

"...You're unusually honest."

"If I think I'm wrong, I'll apologize. I intended to show you properly that I'm not such an unreasonable woman."

Winking at Subaru, who was overreacting and feeling awkward, was a girl in black clothes. When trying to speak of that girl, the first things that catch the eye are her demonic beauty that steals the eyes and ensnares the heart, and the startling fact that only two colors express that beauty.

Her long, snow-white hair reaches down to her waist, and her slender, supple limbs are wrapped in a black dress resembling mourning clothes. There was a decadent allure in her figure as she sat in the chair with her long legs crossed, and there was an abnormally enticing charm in the way her thin lips swallowed the liquid from the cup. It was a demonic countenance that one could intuitively sense would lead to unavoidable destruction if touched by force—nevertheless, she was the Witch of calamity who brought about the end, of whom legends remained of those who never ceased to challenge her.

That is the true identity of the white girl dressed in black, Echidna, who entered into a contract with Subaru.

"—Stare."

"—? What is it? Staring so intently at my face. Is there something on it?"

"Yeah, eyes, a nose, a mouth, ears, and hair."

"...I wonder why. Even though you're saying something obvious, I feel strangely insulted."

Echidna frowned at Subaru's response, looking dissatisfied. Subaru raised his hand to her, saying "Wait, wait," and—

"I didn't mean it as an insult or anything. I just thought, for all the rumors, a Witch isn't really that much of a freak of nature after all."

Although he had used various expressions like decadent and perverse earlier, when actually talking to her like this, she was a normal girl, albeit one with some strong quirks, whom one might even call friendly. Of course, he didn't deny that she had parts that were distorted in a Witch-like way.

"Ho-ho, that's quite a rare opinion."

And at Subaru's impression, Echidna relaxed her lips somewhat enjoyably. She placed her cup on the table, recrossed her long legs, and brushed back her white hair.

"Even so, I am one of the Witches who left various legends in different regions four hundred years ago. To catch me and call me normal is something I can't just overlook."

"You've started saying things like a middle schooler who longs for special treatment."

"If you add 'to you,' it gets quite close to my feelings. But that's not all. I should have told you that when we first met, too."

"Ah—, what was it? I think you said you have a look that makes people who aren't resistant vomit at first sight."

"If you only cut out that part, I feel like my character image is being severely misunderstood."

The dignified aura of a Witch vanished, and Echidna sulked at Subaru's response. Subaru responded appropriately with a "There, there," and set about correcting the diverted topic. That said, the content of their talk was sufficient for a morning routine.

"This isn't the time to be stuck in idle talk. I'm going to head back to reality quickly."

"Are you leaving already? No one will complain if you kill a little more time here. As you know, the time spent here does not affect reality. If it's any consolation, you can stay as long as... I, too, am hoping for that."

Echidna said this to stop Subaru, who had stood up from his chair and was stretching. At the Witch's sweet words, Subaru cracked his neck and said,

"You're right. Honestly, I know I'd be saved by the option of staying here for a long time, but..."

Cutting his words there, Subaru looked down at Echidna as he turned around. The Witch's black eyes and Subaru's black eyes met and intertwined directly. But—,

"I have no intention of spoiling myself with you like that. You should know that's not the kind of contract we have."

"Honestly, you're so stubborn. Even though I intend to be the only accomplice in this world who can share your suffering and sadness."

"—I have no intention of entrusting my suffering or sadness to anyone. This is mine alone, and keeping it as mine alone is the reason I contracted with you. Right?"

To Echidna, who shrugged with a sulky face, Subaru asserted in a low voice. In response, Echidna lowered her eyes and put her lips to her cup in silence. It was an expression of intent that she had run out of words to argue and had lost the reason to continue the conversation. Seeing that, Subaru turned his back to her and stepped toward the bottom of the hill. But before that, he stopped and looked back at the Witch.

"By the way, you didn't deny that you're peeking at me twenty-four-seven earlier, did you?"

"...I believe I said 'even if'?"

"In my linguistic understanding, saying 'even if that were the case' is a context that implicitly admits it is a fact."

"――――"

"――――"

At Subaru's pursuit, Echidna gave a small sigh. Then she put her lips to her cup again. It was an expression of intent that she had lost the reason to continue the conversation—and,

"You'd better not think I'll back down if you do that. You're stopping the peeking when there's an ethical problem, right? Hey, you are, right?"

"Of course. I'm not so vulgar as to peek even at the scenes where you're bathing or relieving yourself. However, as long as I can't be certain that nothing will happen in those defenseless moments, as your contractor, I cannot look away. This is merely an act out of a sense of duty as the 'Witch of Greed' who observes the covenant..."

"From now on, I'm taking off the pendant when I go to the bathroom or the toilet."

Saying that to the Witch who was making excuses at a rapid pace, Subaru descended the hill while biting back his sense of shame. At the bottom of the hill, at the point where Subaru first appeared in the grassland, a door had appeared before he knew it. A single, independent door; it was the only door that allowed entry and exit to the outside world.

"――――"

Arriving in front of the door and placing his hand on the doorknob, Subaru looked back for no particular reason. Then, on top of the hill, Echidna, standing restlessly while holding down her hair fluttering in the wind, was looking down at his back. Noticing Subaru's gaze looking up at her, she hesitated for a moment, and eventually waved her hand slightly at Subaru. Without giving any reply, Subaru left only a sigh and passed through the door.

—Immediately after, his consciousness, which had been trapped in the dream world, was released and returned to reality.

"――――"

When his consciousness returned to reality, Subaru was standing still in the middle of his room, still clutching the black crystal stone. Exhaling a long breath, he turned his eyes to the magic clock crystal above the door. The color was green; there was no change in its glow. The proof was that the time spent in the dream world was only a few seconds in real time.

"Still, I can't get used to it..."

Visiting the dream world and spending time at the tea party with Echidna is a daily morning routine. However, returning to reality from there, the sense of incongruity created by the gap between perceived time and actual time does not easily settle in his brain. It is a sensation different from 'Return by Death.' Subaru sighed at that. Then,

『How about reversing your thinking and getting used to not being used to it? It's only a consolation, but it might make things a little easier, you know?』

As if whispered in his ear, the voice of the Witch he had just bid farewell to was heard. It wasn't actually whispered in his ear. That voice was the Witch's thought waves flowing in through the crystal stone hanging from his neck, via their contract. Even without going to the dream world, a path is connected between Subaru and Echidna by their contract. Therefore, the Witch is able to speak directly to Subaru from the dream world like this. However—,

"I told you not to talk to me thoughtlessly when there's no business."

『There's no one around. I don't think it would hurt to pay some attention to me when you're alone in your room.』

"What if I accidentally do the same in front of people and get thought of as some cringey guy talking to someone invisible?"

『I feel like that evaluation is a bit late now. Besides, it's not rare for spirit users to talk to their contracted spirits via thought waves. Talking to yourself won't stand out that much.』

"In the case of spirit users, it's thought waves on both sides. In my case, you're thought waves and I'm talking to myself. From an outsider's perspective, I'm the cringier one."

『Yes, yes, I understand. As you wish, I'll stay quiet except when necessary. To try and make a Witch do your bidding, you really are quite the domineering husband.』

"S-H-U-T-U-P-A-L-R-E-A-D-Y."

『Good grief. Please feel free to call on me if you have any business, Sir Contractor.』

When he mixed anger into his tone, Echidna withdrew after leaving behind some theatrical words. Still, the Witch must be peeking at the world through the pendant, but for now, he pushed that thought out of his head and let out the sigh that had become a habit. From now on, he would make sure to shove the pendant into his back pocket or something during the timing of the bath and the toilet. —It was just when he thought that.

"—Subaru-sama, are you awake?"

The room door was knocked from the outside, announcing the arrival of the usual wake-up time. At the soft voice addressed to him, Subaru cleared his throat slightly. Then, placing his hand on his cheek and being conscious of making the smile he had confirmed in the mirror, he gave a spiritless reply, "Oiyo—." Receiving that, the door was slowly opened, and a girl entered the room.

"Good morning, Subaru-sama. It's a pleasant morning again today."

The one who said that with a smile was a girl in a maid uniform—Subaru's personal maid, Petra Leyte.

She, who was still a young bud when they first met, had entered her growth period and was now in the process of blooming beautifully. Compared to before, her height and limbs had grown, and the lovely maid uniform suited her more and more. Her movements and behavior were by no means inferior to her charming outfit. Originally, she was a girl with a good memory and quick wit. The current Petra had reached the level of a first-class maid.

At the flowery age of fourteen—at Petra's thoroughly grown-up smile, Subaru also responded with a smile.

"Good morning to you too, Petra. A pleasant morning... I agree. Being able to see Petra's cute face from the morning, I'm also very satisfied."

"Oh, Subaru-sama, saying things like that again. ...But there are clouds over the sky beyond the forest, and it looks like the weather might break from the afternoon. I have plans to go shopping in town today, so I hope it stays clear."

Tilting her head at Subaru's greeting, Petra muttered as she softly turned her eyes toward the window. A faint trace of childishness remained at the end of her words, and Subaru let out a small chuckle.

"Ah, no, Subaru-sama."

"No, no, just now, the shell of a fine maid was cracking a little bit. It's very Petra-like."

"Geez, please stop treating me like a child. I've become a full-fledged adult. I've been doing maid work for two years now... I'm a professional."

Shaking her reddish-brown hair, Petra blushed with embarrassment and pouted her lips. At the sight of his lovely younger-sister-like figure, Subaru patted the girl's head while still laughing. At the sensation, Petra narrowed her eyes happily, and then let out a regretful sigh.

"So, what will you do this morning? It's still early for breakfast, but..."

"Ah—, for now, I'll just finish my usual daily routine, and if it seems okay, we'll all have breakfast together. If it's tough... for now, just as usual."

"...Yes."

Lowering her eyes slightly at Subaru's answer, Petra made a difficult face. It was painful to let such a lovely girl make that kind of face, so Subaru scratched his head and said,

"Anyway, thanks for today too. For going out of your way to come wake me up."

"――――"

"Petra?"

"Subaru-sama, you're really clumsy in that way."

Evaluating Subaru, who was bad at changing the subject, Petra sighed as if exasperated. Then the girl shook her head and, making a smile like a blooming flower, said,

"No—, this is also my job. Besides, Subaru-sama never oversleeps, so it's very easy. ...Even though it would be fine if you were a bit more slovenly."

"Hmm?"

"It's nothing. Well then, see you later. Excuse me."

Finishing her morning duty as Subaru's wake-up call, Petra gracefully pinched her skirt and performed a curtsy. And, while being seen off by Subaru, she left the room. The door closed, and Subaru nodded with satisfaction at Petra's growth.

"Petra's become quite impressive too. As her older-brother-figure, I'm proud."

『It seems she doesn't think of you as an older-brother-figure, though.』

"...You just said you'd stay quiet except when necessary. I guess Lady Witch breaks promises quite easily."

At the interjection to his monologue, Subaru was exasperated by the Witch's lack of patience in being unable to endure the silence. But, immediately leaving behind a sigh of resignation, he turned his eyes to the door where Petra had disappeared.

"I'm that girl's older brother. And Petra is my cute, cute little sister. That's why I'll make sure she becomes happy. —That's a certainty."

『"I'll make sure she becomes happy," is it? ...Unexpectedly, those might be terrifying words.』

Echidna said that to Subaru's firm tone, not in a teasing way. Ignoring the Witch's words, Subaru stretched lightly and left the room. The mansion's hallway was chilled by the early morning air, and Subaru shivered as he began to walk, cutting through that cold air with his shoulders.

—Because he had to get to his daily 'routine.'

For these past few months, a fierce 'deathmatch' has been unfolding in the morning at the Roswaal mansion.

However, calling it a 'deathmatch' might be offensive to the parties involved. But, for Subaru, who watches the struggle from the side, there is no other way to describe it than a 'deathmatch,' as such intense exchanges are being traded. In fact, there is no doubt that at least one of the two clashing parties is attacking with the intent to kill. But sadly, the other party facing that ferocious fighting spirit parries it lightly. The difference in skill existing between the two was hopelessly clear to anyone's eyes. Of course, that was precisely why such a dangerous daily routine was being continued every morning.

"Ru, AAAAA—!!"

Howling, a kick foot explodes with the force to blow away the lawn of the courtyard. Stepping in and gaining fierce propulsion, a golden shadow charges straight ahead, striking the enemy shadow with beast claws. It was a ruthless blow that would easily tear through thick iron plates and turn a human body into minced meat—the beast claws became countless barrages, raging and attacking as if to seal the prey's escape. However—,

"It's a pity, but your opening is large."

"Ga, A—!?"

The shadow dodges the downpour of fatal beast claw flurries with minimal evasion. Immediately after, with a shout, a long leg leaped up, piercing the beast's torso from directly below. A cry of agony rose at that blow, and the body of the attacker was launched straight up—

"Ga, zuo—!?"

—or so it should have been, but it didn't happen. The body being thrust straight up was intercepted by a heel swung down as if to meet it, and crashed into the ground. The impact created a circular crater in the lawn ground, and the beast fell completely silent in a spread-eagle position, unable to move.

"Shall we continue?"

"—Ah, ooh."

The victor asks the loser, who is groaning, about his intention to continue. It was not sarcasm or irony, but words thrown purely at his fighting spirit. That was exactly why it deeply wounded the loser's soul. That's why—,

"You're probably doing it unconsciously, but it's only coming off as sarcasm. Have some mercy and stop it."

Interrupting the scene where the outcome was already decided, Subaru called out while scratching his head. At those words, the young man who had been looking down at the opponent lying spread-eagle turned around and smiled. The young man with flaming red hair raised his hand to Subaru and said,

"Ya, good morning, Subaru. You're early this morning too."

"Not as much as you guys. Even so, don't you get tired of it, or rather, don't you ever learn... You don't have to go along with him so faithfully, Reinhard."

"His ambition to become stronger isn't wrong. And it's also a fact that he's getting stronger day by day. I think the time when he catches up to me will come before long."

"Is that so? ...I can't really imagine it, though."

Reinhard's—the red-haired young man's—words didn't have the atmosphere of a joke or humility. Subaru squinted at his self-and-other evaluation and looked at the person still lying on the ground.

Lying there is a boy with short golden hair. He is gasping painfully and is still in an incapacitated state, but the fighting spirit dwelling in those green eyes has not faded at all. However, his body does not follow his will, and he is just gnashing his teeth regretfully. Having an understanding of that feeling of regret, Subaru reached out his hand to the boy.

"Here, can you stand, Garfiel? Don't be so down. You..."

"...Don't try to touch me so casually. I can stand without borrowin' your hand."

Brushing away Subaru's reaching hand, the golden-haired boy—Garfiel—bared his fangs. But it was clear to anyone's eyes that it was a bluff. Pain was thick on his face, and his breathing was rough. However, Subaru couldn't be immature enough to make fun of that bravado. Lightly shaking the arm that was brushed away, Subaru sighed at Garfiel, who was picking himself up, and said,

"Well, if you say so, do as you like. But before breakfast, take a bath and wash off the sweat and mud. Otherwise, Ram will hate you."

"...Shut up. I know that without you tellin' me."

Frowning at Subaru's bitter advice, Garfiel stood up while grimacing. His knees were still shaking, but even so, he wasn't unable to walk. Recovering with extraordinary resilience, Garfiel glared at Reinhard, who was standing coolly.

"Next time, I won't lose."

"I'm looking forward to it."

At Reinhard's answer, which lacked even a hint of a lie, Garfiel snorted. Then the limping boy, when passing by Subaru's side, glanced at him and—

"—Keh."

—with a resentful click of his tongue, he withdrew from the courtyard to the mansion. Watching that scene with a wry smile, Subaru shook his head in exasperation. His pride was wounded, and there must be some awkwardness. Even so, it would be fine if he washed away the ill feelings along with the sweat before breakfast. Truly, he is at a difficult age.
