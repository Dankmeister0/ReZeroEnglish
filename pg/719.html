—It was a single mismatched button.

With one slip of the gears, the story twists greatly, and the rails of fate easily change direction. If the intended flow changes, the torrent of destiny tosses people about like fallen leaves.

Even so, if there is a way to reach the unchanging far shore without surrendering... It must be only when an unshakeable, ironclad desire manages to utterly deceive that heavenly mandate.

That once-in-a-lifetime lie is the very meaning of the man's existence.

"Hah, hah...!"

Gasping for breath, the girl ran with all her might, pulling a young child by the hand. Desperately, with everything she had, she was still slow. The girl had a disabled leg, and this was an unfamiliar land. —No, if she were to be honest, this was an unfamiliar world to her. A place she didn't recognize, circumstances she didn't understand, a position she knew nothing about. And—,

"Uu! Uu, aa-uu!"

She didn't even know the name of the child who continued to let out wordless cries as she pulled him along.

"—"

Biting her lip hard, the girl pushed away the weakness creeping into her mind and looked forward. Certainly, she understood nothing right now. However, there were things she did know. That she must not let go of this child's hand, and that she must keep running. Amidst all the unknowns, her instincts screamed with the urge to flee. Escaping was the only means allowed to her to express her will to resist.

"That person is...!"

Feeling the pain in her lungs from shortness of breath, she pictured one person in her chaotic mind. A black-haired boy with a sharp gaze, an existence that brought about negative impulses and strange, deep emotions—,

"—Ah, as I thought, a demi-human. Rare."

"—!"

Suddenly, she shuddered at the carefree voice that interrupted her thoughts and snapped her head around. Reflected in the girl's pale blue eyes was a one-eyed woman with an eyepatch running—no, flying—alongside her. A beautiful woman with brown skin mostly exposed to the wind and the beast ears characteristic of a dog-person. At the appearance of the woman flying through the air with a long tree branch in one hand, the child being pulled by the girl also let out a scream. The moment she heard that scream, the girl reflexively tried to swing her free hand to ward the woman off. However—,

"—? You missed. Too bad."

The swing of her arm, thrown off balance, was easily caught, and she was pulled in instead. Their faces were so close their noses almost touched, and in the woman's red eye—the one not covered by the eyepatch—the girl saw her own face reflected. A face she didn't recognize. —A girl's face that didn't even know itself, let alone the world.

"I was told to catch you."

"—Ah."

Immediately after recognizing her own face, the woman's finger was tapped lightly against the center of her chest, and the girl felt a thin shock from the fingertip race through her body. It very easily drained the strength from her limbs, forcing her to her knees on the spot.

"Uu!"

"That child too."

Beside the collapsed girl, the brave child lunged at the woman. But the woman caught him as easily as twisting a baby's arm, and the child received the same shock to his forehead. "Aa-uu?" he cried as his strength left him and he flopped down. As a result, the girl and the child's escape drama came to an abrupt end.

"You... are..."

"Don't worry, I won't do anything bad... I don't know. Sorry."

The woman apologized to the girl, who squeezed out her voice while crawling toward the dizzy child. But it was by no means a comfort, and there was no feeling of being saved by the apology. Whether to take it as sincerity or clumsiness, the girl could not choose either. Reality simply drifted away, just like the child who had lost consciousness before her.

"Let's go, demi-human girl."

"I... am..."

She felt her body being picked up by the woman, but she couldn't resist. Beside her fading consciousness, the girl's lips trembled weakly. Being called a demi-human didn't ring a bell. That said, any other name—,

"—Rem."

That was the name uttered by the black-haired boy, enveloped in a terribly foul stench. It was probably some kind of illusion. —The fact that, to her empty self, that name felt somehow familiar to her ears.

"Kah-kah-kah! Being young is a scary thing! Even for me, I've never seen such reckless, impossible bravado before. Amazing, amazing."

The hoarse voice laughing boisterously was accompanied by a steel hand-blade piercing through his heart. Witnessing the sight of his own heart being plucked from his chest as the hand-strike entered from his back and transfixed his body, Subaru died instantly amidst excruciating pain and shock.

At the new starting point of 'Return by Death,' Subaru was already alone. —Being alone here might have been the original mistake. What is necessary? What possibility must be obtained? Where is the checkpoint he must reach?

Subaru, who had to cross an incredibly thin and unreliable tightrope to reach the other side no matter what, what exactly did he need to reach?

In a situation where he had to draw attention, if he moved carelessly, he would be blocked by powerhouses he couldn't possibly handle with his own strength and would have his life taken in an instant. There were many enemies. And not a single one showed mercy. —Those who had no hesitation in killing their opponents were blocking Subaru's path. To break through this, to overcome it, he had to find a thin, small gap of possibility—,

Running across the neatly trimmed lawn, Subaru headed in a different direction from any of the paths that had been closed off before, and there he found a luxurious detached palace. Flashy and beautiful, but compared to the castle Subaru had been trial-and-erroring in until just now, it was less gaudy, leading him to hope the security might be somewhat lighter. He hoped to at least gain a single piece of information there. With that intent, Subaru broke a window in an unpopulated spot, unlocked it, and tumbled into the palace. The next moment—,

"—Fool."

"Ogoff!"

After all, in just the last ten minutes or so, Subaru had repeatedly met his end by being killed by completely different opponents in different ways. It was no wonder he braced himself for this to end in the same failure amidst the pain. But—,

"You, how did you slip past the security and break in this far?"

The shock of 'Death' he expected did not fall upon him; instead, there was a question. While grimacing in pain, Subaru saw the person who threw the question at him—the person who had intercepted Subaru as he broke into the palace—from where he lay sprawled on the floor.

"————"

Standing there was a strikingly handsome man with black hair and black eyes, possessing an incredibly sharp and cold impression. Dressed in crimson attire whose high quality was apparent at a glance, the man looked down at Subaru with a sharp, hawk-like gaze. His consciousness stolen by that glare, Subaru froze as if pinned down. —However, it wasn't just the man's gaze that froze Subaru's movements.

"Did you not hear my question? To return not a single answer is an act of irreverence."

With a solemn voice and manner of speech, the handsome man was about to coldly decide Subaru's fate. However, Subaru's consciousness was captured more by the strange occurrence beside the man than by that cruel fact—specifically, by the mysterious thing inside the full-length mirror placed there. Inside the mirror, the reflection of the black-haired man was different. —Reflected there was a man taller than the black-haired one, with white hair, white clothes, and a white mask.

Subaru gasped at the strangeness, and the real handsome man closed one eye and clicked his heel. Then, as if giving a final ultimatum, he issued the question once more.

"There will not be a third time. Answer my question with care."

Subaru blinked at the repeated demand, his consciousness pulled back to reality. At that moment, the figure of the white man in the mirror was nowhere to be seen, and there was only the handsome man looking down at him with an incredibly intimidating attitude. Facing that, Subaru took a breath and then said:

"—My name is Natsuki Subaru."

It was an answer inappropriate for any of the questions the man had asked. Subaru himself, even looking back later, couldn't think of a proper reason why he had introduced himself by his real name without lying. It was likely just an intuitive answer produced by a mind that had gone haywire after repeating irrational and absurd 'Deaths' many times in a short period.

Hearing Subaru's answer, which could have easily rubbed the man the wrong way, the handsome man—,

"—The 'Little Girl User' of Lugunica?"

He muttered, furrowing his well-shaped brows and wearing an expression of confusion that didn't suit his arrogant face—a face that looked as if it held every event in this world in the palm of its hand.

—That was the first mismatched button.

The encounter between Natsuki Subaru, who had been sent to the Sacred Empire of Vollachia in an unexpected way, and 'Vincent Vollachia,' the pinnacle of the nation of the Sword Wolf—two men who deceive fate, whose destinies were never meant to cross.

"I don't care about that one. In fact, I'd prefer if that one stayed asleep forever."

Apparently, she didn't like Subaru's answer, but he wasn't sorry because it was the truth. He couldn't pretend otherwise. —Unlike Rem, who was brave, harmless, hardworking, and kind, that girl was a great villain who had piled up unforgivable sins.

She seemed to be asleep now, but she was one of the enemies who had tormented Subaru and made him taste hell during the battle at the Pleiades Watchtower. It was a mystery why she, who was supposed to be an existence without a physical body, had manifested with a real body, but it couldn't be for anything good. In any case—,

"Thank you for not hurting Rem. You are..."

"Arakia."

"Thank you, Arakia-chan! I owe you one!"

Subaru bowed deeply and expressed his gratitude to the girl—Arakia. She probably didn't have a good impression of Subaru because of how he treated Louis just now, but Subaru felt a strong sense of gratitude and debt toward Arakia. And the one who ordered Arakia to do so was—,

"You've been a great help. Um..."

He was the person who had intercepted Subaru when he broke into the palace and had almost executed him for his insolence, and if what he just said was true, he was the supreme authority of the Empire.

"Arakia-chan?"

"It's true. ...A little bit of a lie?"

"A little bit of a lie?"

"Arakia, do not speak unnecessary words. —If you wish to take back what you truly desire, act so as not to offend my mood."

Arakia, responding to Subaru's look of confirmation, was rebuked by the man—Vincent—and nodded with a slightly dissatisfied face. Arakia then placed Rem and Louis on the reception sofa to let them sleep. Subaru wanted to separate the two sleeping girls as much as possible, but since he wasn't in a position or atmosphere to assert that, he endured it by just clenching his fists.

After all, this was the Sacred Empire of Vollachia. The culture and common sense of this different world that Subaru had spent a year learning were different from the very soil here; it was the Imperial Capital where a person of noble standing from the neighboring country of the Kingdom of Lugunica resided.

"...I'd prefer if you called me Natsuki Subaru. As you can see, I don't have that lovely, charming, pretty girl who suits that title by my side right now."

"She's a girl who is neither lovely, charming, nor pretty."

Subaru responded bluntly to Arakia, who pointed at Louis and asked. Then, turning his gaze back to Vincent, the Emperor closed one of his black eyes and said,

"Speak, Natsuki Subaru."

"...No matter how many times you ask, it won't change much. We were at the 'Sage's' tower at the eastern edge of Lugunica. Various things happened at the tower, but I'll skip that... In the end, the 'Witch of Envy' partially overflowed from the shrine near the Great Waterfall and swallowed us. Then, when I realized it, I had been sent to the grounds of this castle..."

"...Sounds like a lie. A lie?"

"I think it sounds incredibly suspicious even as I'm saying it, but on the flip side, I have no reason to utter a story that is nothing but lies, so I want you to believe me...!"

He had listed the facts, but Arakia rightly doubted him. However, Subaru was the one who wanted an explanation of the circumstances. He had just finished the battle with the 'Gluttonies' at the Pleiades Watchtower, lost someone he didn't want to lose under the name of a 'Trial,' and just as his frayed heart was showing signs of calming down, this happened.

"Right now, Emilia-tan, Beako, and the others must be worried sick... To think we're suddenly in the Empire, and on the grounds of the castle in the Imperial Capital at that."

"Contrary to your skill in infiltration, you are currently the most likely candidate for a spy with low-level excuses. If there is anything noteworthy, it is that you introduced yourself as Natsuki Subaru."

"...It's not a lie, it's the truth, but did that have much meaning?"

"That the 'Little Girl User' is the knight of one of the Royal Selection candidates is a story known even in the Empire. The threat of the Witch Cult and the Three Great Demon Beasts is not something Vollachia views from the opposite shore."

"I see..."

"A non-aggression pact has been signed between the Empire and the Kingdom. I could even claim that your very existence has broken that pact... but..."

"No, please spare me from that."

It would be unbearable if Subaru's careless self-introduction caused the two countries to enter a state of war. In the first place, this was truly an unfortunate misunderstanding, an unexpected accident. Subaru had no means to explain it, and it was pathetic that his only move was to desperately plead that the lie-like facts were the truth—,

"Oh my, oh my, isn't that a bit too much of a threat, Your Excellency? I don't think we have the luxury of picking a fight with the Kingdom right now."

"—! W-Who is it!?"

"I have come in accordance with the mandate of heaven, Your Excellency."

"I told you, didn't I? It's in accordance with the mandate of heaven. I rarely get a turn, so I have to act properly as a 'Stargazer' when the opportunity arises."

"If you ask if I'm certain, I have no way of confirming since the Kingdom is outside my jurisdiction... but regarding that, I suspect Your Excellency had reached a similar inference."

"Here."

Subaru, who had panicked and dropped his polite speech, wasn't rebuked; instead, Vincent tapped the floor with his toe. For a moment, Subaru didn't connect that to the answer to his question and blinked. But he soon understood the meaning,

"Here... you mean, an internal conflict within the Empire?"

"Yes. Over the throne of this Empire, the Emperor and the rebels shall clash."

"————"

Confirmed with the fact of a war—a civil war—Subaru was momentarily speechless. Reaching out a hand to the speechless Subaru, Vincent declared. With the gaze and face of an absolute powerhouse, like a bird of prey, the Vollachian Emperor declared:

"Regardless of the circumstances, if you wish to survive, you shall accompany me, Natsuki Subaru. —To ensure that the Empire remains as it should be, in the hands of none other than Vincent Vollachia."

—This has truly turned into a bad situation.

"————"

He had encountered many irrational situations before. The hardships at the Pleiades Watchtower just before this could be said to be the most times he'd been pummeled by irrationality. Even so, those still had continuity. One could say there was a path where the situation gradually worsened until he was pushed into an irrational state.

Yet, this one lacked that. Subaru coming to the Vollachian Empire was nothing but a pure irrationality without any warning. It wouldn't have been strange if he had been sent not to the Imperial Capital of Vollachia, but to a more remote area, or even to the Kararagi City-States or the Holy Kingdom of Gusteko, yet he was sent here. If this wasn't called irrationality, what was—,

"Ah."

"Uu, mm..."

Suddenly, Arakia, noticing something, spoke up, followed by Rem stirring on the sofa. Recognizing that as a sign of awakening, Subaru turned to Rem as if spring-loaded. He knelt beside her on the sofa and waited for the eyelids fringed with lashes to slowly open. He saw himself reflected dimly in her pale blue eyes, and—,

"Rem! Thank goodness, are you okay? Does it hurt anywhere? Are you injured, or do you feel sick? If there's anything wrong, immediately..."

"—! Why are you here!"

"Gwo-hway!"

Even as he worried about her condition, the awakened Rem suddenly pushed Subaru away. Blown back by that unreserved force, Subaru flew along a trajectory where Ubilk happened to be. "Aah-ray?" Ubilk said, rounding his eyes as he was caught in the collateral damage of the flying Subaru, and the two of them rolled to the corner of the room in a heap. Ignoring Subaru and the other, Rem sat up on the sofa and scanned her surroundings,

"A-Where is that child... wait, you! From before!"

"...Your Excellency?"

"—. This is the Emperor's detached palace. It is far too noisy."

Seeing Arakia, Rem sharpened her guard. At Rem's gaze, Arakia turned her sleepy eyes toward Vincent, and the Emperor let out a quiet sigh. Just as the Emperor said, it was a situation that lacked a sense of tension—,

"Uu—?"

Louis, who woke up late, rubbed her eyes and tilted her head curiously, which was the scene that lacked the most tension.

—Illegal entry into the detached palace where His Imperial Excellency Vincent Vollachia resided, in the 'Crystal Palace' located at the northern end of Lupgana, the Imperial Capital of the Sacred Empire of Vollachia.

That was the crime Subaru and Rem had committed, which would surely have seen them executed about seven times. If judged by Imperial law, Subaru and the others were confirmed criminals, but the reason they weren't executed wasn't because of a stylish arrangement by a merciful and compassionate Emperor—of course not.

"As for me, I would prefer to sequester personnel like you, who are difficult to handle, until the most pressing matters are resolved, and exclude the option of even adding you to the agenda."

"You say that very clearly, that I'm a nuisance."

"Are you not aware of it yourself? In this situation, to introduce yourself by the name of a knight involved in Lugunica's Royal Selection, regardless of its truth, is something that invites unnecessary calamity."

"...Unfortunately, in the Kingdom, my name is well-known enough that people wave to me on the street."

Even as he said that, Subaru was reflecting on whether it had been reckless to introduce himself so carelessly. However, if he had said or done anything other than giving the name Natsuki Subaru in that place, he might have been killed by Vincent without question. Since Vincent had shown a willingness to listen, confronting him must have been the right choice.

"The others were coming for my life without question..."

"Rather, I am impressed that your head is still so well-connected to your torso. ...The fact that you headed straight for the detached palace where His Excellency was also plays a part in my suspicion."

Regarding that point, it was a process of elimination because all other routes led straight to a Bad End, but it was obvious that this squinty-eyed old man wouldn't be convinced by that explanation. Imperial Prime Minister, Berstetz Fondalfon. —That was the name and title of this squinty-eyed old man, and it was a clear reason for him to be wary of Subaru, an outsider.

"I know it doesn't matter what I say, but I don't have any schemes or motives. If I'm being honest, I want to go back to Lugunica as soon as possible."

"As you say, it doesn't matter what you say. In the current situation where cross-border travel has been restricted, it is also difficult to commit the folly of sending you back to that land while the suspicion of being an agent has not been cleared."

"Flowingly preaching the logical argument..."

At Berstetz's uncompromising words, Subaru sighed and slumped his shoulders. The location was a corner of the Imperial Capital Lupgana, the mansion where Berstetz resided. Subaru's person, having received a lenient sentence for the crime of illegal entry into the detached palace, was currently placed in the Prime Minister's mansion. Berstetz, who was held in high regard by the Emperor, Vincent, did not relax his guard against Subaru at all, yet he correctly fulfilled the duty of taking custody of him. Subaru, while feeling the prickliness of Berstetz's unconcealed suspicion, had no major complaints about his treatment.

If he had to say, his only regret was that he had been successfully played by Vincent's intentions and was unable to take active steps to return to the Kingdom. Or perhaps, the biggest reason their persons were entrusted to Berstetz's mansion, which had security even more formidable than the Crystal Palace or its detached palace, was—,

"—I'm back-cha."

After finishing the interrogation from Berstetz, on his way back to his assigned room, Subaru witnessed a flying dragon landing in the mansion's garden with a swirl of wind, and a small figure dismounting from its back. Standing there was a girl with sky-blue hair and two black horns—a dragon-person with a lovely appearance. Contrary to her cute looks, she was a dragon-person with unbelievable combat power.

"Madelyn-chan."

"...Is it you-cha, human. Don't call a dragon '-chan' so casually-cha."

The returning dragon-person girl, Madelyn, noticed Subaru calling her name and gave a small snort. Subaru often passed Madelyn, who seemed to be under Berstetz's protection just like them, in this mansion and had chances to see her. However, every time her attitude was like this, prickly, so he wouldn't say they had built a good relationship. She was prickly not just toward Subaru, but toward everyone in the mansion—,

"First General Madelyn, how was it? Those who seek the release of First General Goz..."

"I kicked them around-cha. I'm telling you, dragons have no obligation to consider your trivial circumstances-cha, so I literally kicked them around-cha."

"It does not matter. If His Imperial Excellency ordered you to suppress them, and you did what was judged necessary because you were needed for that, then that is the correct answer for the Empire."

"Hmph, what a boring old man-cha."

Madelyn shrugged at Berstetz's answer. On her back was a weapon—a boomerang—nearly as large as her small frame. There was blood here and there on the boomerang, and there was no doubt that the blood she couldn't wipe off was related to the 'suppression' the two were talking about.

"First General Madelyn Eschart and First General Goz Ralfon..."

The one who appeared was a black-haired handsome man wearing a mask modeled after an ogre on his face—ironically, the one confronting Chisha, whose profession was wearing 'masks,' was the true and only Emperor of this Sacred Empire of Vollachia, Vincent Vollachia himself.

Having repelled many and encountered numerous hardships, Vincent returned to his castle. The return of the Emperor, who was not physically superior and had no one to rely on in his position of being driven from the throne, and whose only weapons were truly his own intellect and the power of his words.

The pinnacle of the Empire, Chisha Gold's master, was asking if his wish had come true.

"After taking everything, do you still seek something from me? You have a greedier side than I estimated. —But it is a deception."

Even so, at the point where the fatigue of the days he spent struggling to reclaim the throne was comparable to when he was handling the heavy duties of the Emperor, Vincent's way of being was too neglectful of himself.

Priscilla Benedict. For what, for whom, why. The wise Emperor, in response to what had happened, trembled his voice as if seeking an answer. However, without Chisha's words, the Emperor would surely not be able to reach that answer. It was just as he had told Vincent just now.

Looking down at the board, thinking about things efficiently, and defining the value of all pieces uniformly. An expression that many would likely misinterpret. The Emperor, who often omitted words because he was too wise, was also laconic in his expression.

Probably, he was the only one who could correctly receive the meaning of this expression, having worked with this Emperor for many years.

"Ah, but—"

He found himself hoping, perhaps, or maybe, or just possibly. That there might be someone who would look straight at this man who was a wonderfully wise Emperor and a too-foolish and self-deprecating man.

It couldn't be helped that he thought that.

The man died.

He was Chesha Trim, became Chisha Gold, and then as Vincent Vollachia, the man died.

He died before the master he served with loyalty and the accomplice who had participated in deceiving that master.
