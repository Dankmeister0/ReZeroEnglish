—What dominated his consciousness was an intense "heat" originating from the center of his body.

"Gu, uuuuu! It’s hot!"

Feeling the sensation of the hard ground against his face, Subaru realized he was lying face down. Yet, the limbs he needed to stand up would not move.
It was as if he were no longer himself; the freedom of his physical body had been stripped away.

And yet, the scorching heat burned Subaru without limit, which was no joke.

—Hot, hot hot hot hot hot hot hot.

When he opened his mouth, what spilled out instead of a scream was a large amount of blood.
At the end of pain and suffering, he felt as if he were drowning in his own overflowing blood, forced to taste the very pinnacle of this world's agony.

—Just what on earth did he do?

An escapist desire to flee from the suffering caused that whimpering thought to spin repeatedly in the back of his mind.

Just what on earth did he do?
He was aware that his way of living had certainly not been one worthy of praise. But that wasn't limited to Subaru. No one should be able to live their daily lives so blamelessly that they could boast to all of humanity. Everyone must have things they feel guilty about, things they regret, things they turned a blind eye to, and things they compromised on.

And yet, why did he alone have to suffer like this?
Why was he alone forced into a fate that every other human was spared from?

"Ah, damn it..."

A murmur leaked through the gaps of the gurgling, overflowing blood.
It was regret, it was hatred for his own helplessness, it was loathing for fate—and,

—It was also a sense of exasperation toward himself.

"————"

Even after being hurt this much, even after suffering this much, even after facing such death-defying experiences.
Even if his body was scorched by heat, literally carved by sharp pain, and even if his life was threatened.

Because the image of the girl burned into his mind was nothing but a repetition of smiles and a sorrowful look of death.

"I will."

Now, once again, he speaks his determination. He speaks his resolve. He speaks his lingering attachments and regrets.
Seeking a future that would not be reached no matter how many times he challenged it, no matter how many times he crawled around, no matter how many times he prayed.

The "pain" and the "heat" were all distant; the underdog would howl unsightly, just like an underdog should.

"Surely."

The brandished lethal blade ruthlessly approached the dying spark of life just before it was extinguished.
However, even that did not enter his sight. His resolve was already absolute.

—I will save you.

The moment he embraced that wish once more, Natsuki Subaru lost his life.

—Seeing the familiar sight once again, Subaru felt a strange sense of relief and exhaustion.

"I have returned safely, so I can rest easy, I guess."

While gazing at the traffic of the Royal Capital, Subaru used a picked-up tree branch to draw "tally marks" on the ground. After continuing until he was satisfied, he stepped on them to erase them and sighed.

Natsuki Subaru was an ordinary third-year high school truant from the third planet of the solar system, Earth.
Looking at his rough appearance—wearing a tracksuit and sneakers, holding a plastic bag from a convenience store—no one would likely doubt that fact.
However, that logic only applied to a cultural sphere where the meanings of loanwords like tracksuit, sneakers, and convenience store were properly conveyed and values could be shared.
In other words, what he wanted to say was—

"—Being summoned to another world is tougher than I thought."

A lizard as massive as a horse pulled a carriage, kicking up a cloud of dust as it crossed in front of him.
Naturally, such a sight could not be seen in the Earth sphere Subaru knew. If so, was that a horse with special effects makeup, a so-called theme park cast member? He couldn't easily nod to that either.

Because that lizard wasn't the only one; they were overflowing everywhere in the streets and the city, and there were too many people who followed those lizards who had also received special effects makeup.
Humans dressed as humanoid beasts—races like demi-humans or beastmen—numbered at least twenty or thirty within Subaru's field of vision. Of course, there were many general visitors with the same shape and form as Subaru, but they were buried under the violence of individuality that was their outward appearance and didn't really enter his consciousness.

In the first place, he couldn't spend his thoughts on this escapism forever.
Neither the demi-humans nor the lizards were cast members or part of a movie shoot. This was a cultural sphere where this was the norm, and the foreign object here was not them, but Subaru.

—Subaru, who had already spent what felt like over a dozen days here, realized that painfully well.

"...Let's go."

Cutting off his fruitless thoughts, he brushed off his backside and stood up.
Subaru, still holding the picked-up tree branch in his right hand, stepped into the street with a vague gait—not blending into the crowd of the main street, but stepping into the back alley.

Unlike the crowded main street, the back alley sandwiched between tall buildings was sparsely populated. The place, which had become an appearance truly fitting for a back alley, was filled with a silence as if it had been isolated from the outside world just by crossing one street.
To put it simply, even if something happened here, a scream would likely never reach the main street.

Therefore, if Subaru, looking every bit the tourist, wandered into such a place, it was only natural for villains targeting him to show themselves.

"Yo, big brother. Why don't you hang out with us for a bit?"

Turning toward the voice, three figures stood blocking the path.
A lineup prepared to meet all needs—Large, Medium, and Small—Subaru felt their serious research capabilities. He didn't know what they were researching, though.

"————"

Tilting his head, he looked at the path opposite the trio. Behind him, this alley was a dead end; to get to the main street, he had no choice but to let the trio pass.
And as for whether the trio had any intention of letting Subaru out to the main street—

"Hey, hey, what's with that dazed face?"

"He probably doesn't understand the situation. Why don't we teach him?"

At Subaru's sluggish behavior, the trio began to plot their torment with vulgar smiles. From their perspective, Subaru was a complete amateur.
A convenient mark—he couldn't blame them for licking their lips in front of him.

However, that was a complete miscalculation on their part.
It was true that Subaru had never been in a proper fight, nor did he have a hidden past of learning martial arts. Their judgment of him as an amateur was not wrong.

But, if it was only regarding his experience points against this specific trio, Subaru was already a veteran of a hundred battles.

"—Ah?"

Thrusting his arm toward the off-guard giant—whom he had named Ton of the Ton-Chin-Kan trio.
In that arm was the tree branch he had picked up on the street earlier. Its tip wasn't particularly sharp, but it dug into the soft part of the throat and, after a slight resistance, pierced through.

"Hey?"

At that instantaneous event, Ton's eyes widened, and Chin and Kan, standing to the left and right, froze.
With this, Ton was out of the fight. And of the two frozen men, Subaru reached out his free hand toward the one of medium build—Chin—grabbing his hair and ear and swinging him around. He wouldn't let him resist. Using the momentum, he slammed the man's head into the side wall all at once.
The sound of something hard being crushed echoed, and Chin collapsed, leaving a trail of blood on the wall.

Simultaneously, Ton, with the tree branch still stuck in his neck, fell to his knees and collapsed with his eyes rolling back. It was a bad way to fall. Because he fell forward, the branch stabbed even deeper.

With this, two were incapacitated. Only one remained—

"Hi..."

The small man—Kan—was turning pale at the sight of his two companions being defeated in an instant.
If he had abandoned the two and run out to the street just like that, it would have been a contest of leg strength with Subaru, and there might have been a chance of survival.
And yet, Kan looked at the two fallen men and hesitated to flee. Not knowing it was already too late, he wasted the limited one-second grace period.

Idiot. A total idiot. Receive the reward for making such a foolish choice.

"Gu, ga, aaaa..."

Wrapping his hand around the thin neck, Subaru used his strength to pin Kan against the back wall. He lifted the struggling small body while keeping it pinned to the wall. He poured strength into both hands and squeezed.
Lifted until his eyes were at the same height as Subaru's, Kan, who was being strangled, widened his eyes, and his loosely opened mouth gasped for oxygen. However, with his airway forcibly blocked and his freedom stripped away, no salvation would come to him.

"Do you know how many times I've met you guys in total now?"

"Ku, ko..."

"It's eighty-eight times. It's a lucky number, isn't it? Smile."

Glaring at the face that was gradually turning reddish-black and leaking tears and drool, Subaru said.
But, naturally, Kan had no such leeway. Soon, losing even his resistance let alone his leeway, Subaru threw the limp body onto the spot.

Looking down at the three fallen men, just in case, he stepped hard on everyone's necks once. If there was the sensation of the sneaker's heel crushing something hard, he could be at ease for the moment.
Only Ton's neck was thick, so it was necessary to step on it five times thoroughly. He just couldn't get the hang of this no matter how many times he did it. Luckily, there were cases where it was settled with one time each.

"Strangling isn't smart. ...The sensation is disgusting too, so I'll stop doing that."

Based on those points of reflection, Subaru borrowed two knives from Chin's body. Then, he moved the three corpses to the side of the road appropriately and left the back alley as if nothing had happened.

Entering the alley and encountering the Ton-Chin-Kan trio, he had made things happen quite early. He took a little time processing the trio, but it was only a matter of ten-odd seconds.
Subaru hurried through the main street and made his way toward his destination.

"————"

Gazing at the state of the street, he breathed a sigh of relief that he had made it in time.
The main street called the commercial district was also crowded with people shuffling left and right, fulfilling its role in proving the bustle of the Royal Capital, Lugnica.
Even just standing there, the clamor was enough to jump into one's eardrums.
However, the atmosphere of that bustle suddenly changed into a different form.

"—Wait! Jeez! Wait!!"

What cut through the bustle of the main street particularly high was a silver-bell voice that one could fall in love with.
While harboring a desperate atmosphere, that voice, which could not hide the gentleness of its owner's temperament, was directed at a small shadow slipping through the crowd.

"Hehe."

With a cat-like smile, a blonde girl wove through the gaps between people. Something shining was held in her hand, and a sense of having finished a job could be clearly seen.
Then, toward that girl, a pale blue glow—an ice spear—flew.

"—!!"

Surprised by the unexpected attack, the girl dodged the ice spear by jumping and leaping.
That magic attack was unleashed in a street full of people, and sudden confusion arose; the people of the Royal Capital opened the way all at once and raised both hands as if to say they wanted no part in it.
It was a very coordinated movement. Getting caught up in trouble in the Royal Capital was a daily occurrence—well, it might be close to that.

Anyway, running through the wall of people that had opened the way, the person he was looking for appeared.

"————"

The moment he saw "her," Subaru felt as if everything in the world had frozen.
He could no longer feel the wind, the voices of people, or even time; everything in his consciousness was stolen by that form.

Her long silver hair danced, and her jewel-like amethyst eyes shone with strong intent. Slender white limbs, and a mysterious white outfit that looked as if it were tailored for a fairy—

In a world where time had stopped, she alone was exempt from the stillness of time and passed before Subaru.

Her goal was the blonde girl who had slipped through just before—Felt.
Something was stolen from her by Felt, and she would run through the Royal Capital to get it back. And for her, that was a path leading to an unavoidable fate of misfortune.

But, he wouldn't let that happen.
He would never let her be caught by a fate of "death."

"I will surely save you."

Toward her receding back, Subaru made his eighty-eighth vow.
He didn't know how much persuasiveness a vow that continued to be broken like this could have.
He didn't know, but if he didn't give up, if he continued to resist, if he continued to wish to save her—

"Please wait for me. —Satella."

The power to rewind time upon death, "Return by Death."
This was the eighty-eighth time Subaru had used this "Return by Death" power to redo the world.

It was a challenge that was already nearing the milestone of a hundred times, but all of it had been spent to save the silver-haired girl—Satella—from the dead end of fate.

"I've tried this and that, through trial and error... but it doesn't go like it did with the Ton-Chin-Kan trio."

And that was Subaru's conclusion regarding the greatest enemy of this fate—Elsa Granhiert.

The one who requested Felt to steal the item, the insignia, from Satella was that jet-black woman named Elsa. This woman was a dangerous person who repeatedly drove Satella to her death.
Up until now, during the eighty-seven challenges, Subaru had many times rushed to eliminate this Elsa and ensure Satella's safety, but he suffered defeat before Elsa's combat power, which was already in the realm of the inhuman, and had his belly sliced open and died over fifty times.

A sadist who loved to open bellies, Elsa was not an opponent he could win against in a fight.
If it were a group like the Ton-Chin-Kan trio, he could pattern their actions to some extent and devise a certain victory strategy. However, when it came to someone of Elsa's strength, the moment Subaru tried to take action, it wouldn't be strange for his head to be lopped off in the blink of an eye.
In fact, there were many times when it wasn't his head, but his belly that was sliced open in the blink of an eye.

Subaru could not win in a fight. Neither could Felt, Old Man Rom, or Satella.
After receiving that conclusion, the tactic Subaru chose was—

"Wonderful! Wonderful! I'd be happy if you entertained me more!"

"Guaaaa—!!"

Black blades flipped one after another, and every time the wind rose, blood sprayed out.
The number of people falling in the street already exceeded what could be counted on both hands, and the expression of Elsa, who was bathed in the spray of blood, went beyond ecstasy and looked as if she were intermittently tasting a sense of climax.

Despite being in such a gruesome situation, Elsa's allure increased the more she was bathed in blood.
In the past, there was an anecdote about a woman called a vampire who tried to maintain her youth by bathing in the blood of virgins, but the current Elsa looked exactly like that "vampire."

"...This is also a failure, huh."

Sighing, Subaru, lurking on the roof, narrowed his eyes while overlooking the carnage.

The location was a corner of the slums, a particularly desolate section surrounded by ruins, and with a space like a plaza as the stage, the carnage of blood continued to expand.
Those who crossed swords with Elsa and were all falling to her lethal blades were the guards of the Royal Capital.
People who received a report from a well-meaning citizen, discovered the dangerous person Elsa Granhiert, and lost their lives trying to capture her—it was a sight that made his heart ache as a well-meaning citizen.

"I didn't think there was such a difference in strength between ordinary guards and Elsa..."

At this rate, it was like offering sacrifices just to please Elsa.
Subaru's expectation was that if they were people in a position like the guards protecting the Royal Capital, they might be able to strengthen their bodies through some kind of magical power and compete on equal terms with Elsa, but it seemed that was merely a high hope.

As expected, Elsa's strength was in a league of its own even in this world.
His resentment toward Elsa grew even stronger. But no matter how strongly he held his anger, Subaru's power could not stop that vicious woman.
He didn't think Elsa would be defeated by a lucky punch from the guards either.

"It's useless to just keep watching any longer."

He felt bad for the guards who became sacrifices, but he would end this attempt here.
He didn't intend to let up yet, but even those who died here would breathe again in the redone world if Subaru ultimately failed and lost his life.
As a sacrifice for that, he would have them stay dead for now while swallowing his tears. If there was another chance, he wouldn't expect anything from them, so he wanted them to protect the peace of the Royal Capital with peace of mind.

Just as Subaru concluded this and was about to leave the place—

"—Oh."

Stopping her feet, Elsa licked her blood-stained lips while her lethal blade was still raised.
The overflowing madness and killing intent were the best proof that Elsa, bored with the game of massacring guards, had found a new prey. And the one chosen as Elsa's poor sacrifice was—

"—That's enough."

Subaru hallucinated that a red flame was standing there.

"————"

Gulping, rubbing his eyes, and looking again, he was finally able to recognize that it was not a flame, but a person.

It was a young man with red flame-like hair and blue eyes that seemed to contain the clear sky.
He looked slender, but his tall body was supplely trained and wrapped in white clothing, and he carried a single sword with an incredibly grand mounting at his waist.
His features were a divine creation so well-formed that if there were a hundred people, all hundred would look back and be captivated. The reason why he didn't make one feel the violence of beauty that transcended gender was perhaps due to the young man's calm demeanor.

At a glance, he had a presence that made one's soul know he was a being different from the masses—

"Knight Reinhard...!"

"Please step back. She is... the 'Bowel Hunter.' There are already too many victims. I don't want to see any more."

At the trembling voice of a surviving guard, the young man—Reinhard—said while slightly lowering his eyes.
It was a pity for the lives slaughtered like dust, and it was also a righteous indignation toward the murderer named Elsa who had done it.

Even to Subaru, who was watching from a distance, the heart of the young man named Reinhard was visible.
What a man who gets angry for such proper reasons, with such proper thoughts, and with such a proper mentality.

Grieving over death, hating slaughter, regretting his inadequacy, and rewriting regret with conviction.
That was the way of being of that young man called Reinhard—the knight.

"Reinhard van Astrea. Of the 'Sword Saint' bloodline. Wonderful, how wonderful!"

"I feel like I'm being crushed by the weight of such excessive evaluation every day. You are the 'Bowel Hunter,' I presume."

"Yes, that's right. This won't do. What should I do? I have my main job to attend to, but to think I'd run into you in a place like this."

Leaking a hot breath of ecstasy, Elsa directed her heat-moistened eyes toward Reinhard.
On the other hand, Reinhard's expression was nothing but serious, and his blue eyes held only a sense of duty with no playfulness at all.

The two faced each other with polar opposite ideologies, but the conclusion their hearts drew was the same.

That there was no choice but for each to strike down the other and achieve their goal.

"Normally, I would recommend that you surrender, but..."

"In the face of this carnage, you would say that to me? You're kind. To the point of being cruel to me and to them."

"—No, I agree. Showing you mercy here would not be sincere toward their deaths. Therefore, I will not ask you to surrender."

Reinhard slowly shook his head at Elsa's words as she playfully kicked the corpses of the fallen guards.
Then, he reached out his hand to a guard standing by and briefly said, "A sword."

"Please, I beg of you."

When the instructed guard offered a sword, Reinhard gripped it and checked the sensation. At that sight, Elsa furrowed her brows suspiciously and said,

"You're not going to use the sword at your waist? I've heard rumors of the Dragon Sword's sharpness and wanted to feel it for myself."

"Unfortunately, this sword has a nature of judging the opponent it should be drawn against. It's a bit of a troublesome nature, but it seems the sword doesn't take a liking to you. Instead, I shall face you with this."

"Hmm."

Elsa sighed at Reinhard, who took a stance with the sword he had received instead of the sword at his waist.
However, her attitude, which seemed somewhat dissatisfied, was only for a moment. Switching immediately, the woman licked her lips at the anticipation of the battle and blood with the young man standing before her.
And—

"The 'Bowel Hunter,' Elsa Granhiert."

"Of the 'Sword Saint' bloodline, Reinhard van Astrea."

Naming each other, the two became the wind and clashed in the center of the plaza.
The match was decided in a single exchange, just like that.

A slash with nothing unusual about it—it created a vortex of light, and the shockwave destroyed a corner of the slums.
The sword strike of the man called the "Sword Saint" was truly "power" itself.

Natsuki Subaru was watching that scene with his eyes wide open.
Without even knowing the cause of the tears streaming down his cheeks and his trembling knees—

Although it was a development different from what Subaru had planned and expected, it could be said that the greatest goal of defeating Elsa had been achieved.

Reinhard's participation was unexpected, but if his wish was granted, it was all well and good.
Thanks to that, the events surrounding Satella's insignia would be resolved safely. If the trading partner, Elsa, did not join them, Felt and Old Man Rom would not push themselves to secure Satella's insignia. It was also hard to imagine Satella killing the two in a fit of rage.
The story centered around the loot cellar would conclude with the minimum necessary characters.
That was a desirable result for Subaru as well.
Therefore—

"—Why are you helping me?"

"I don't care if you don't trust me. If you're saying it's fine to be surrounded by guards and lose your chance for revenge, then cut me down and run as you like."

"————"

Subaru said as if spitting it out to Elsa, who was crouching against the wall behind him.
Her entire body was covered in blood, her black outfit was torn in places, and her white skin was exposed. However, since painful scars were prominent on her skin, there was not a shred of room to feel shame about it.
In the first place, Subaru had absolutely no interest in Elsa as a woman.
He was merely using the opportunity because he thought she could be useful.

"The guards are looking for you with bloodshot eyes. I've led them in the wrong direction, so it'll take a while before they notice this way. Your wounds?"

"They hurt a lot. It hurts so much, I feel like I might die. Fufu, how wonderful."

"That's a sensibility I don't understand. It'd be a problem if you died, so I want to do something about it."

He was going as far as to risk danger to guide Elsa toward survival.
If she collapsed here, or worse, if Subaru got caught up in the guards' search, the accidentally generated Satella survival route would go up in smoke.
In that case, he would just keep rolling the dice until this result came up again.

"Can you make a path to the southeast of the slums? If you do, I can meet up with my sister. She should have prepared treatment for my wounds and an escape route."

"Sister! Ha, having a sister too is quite thorough and a hopeless story."

It wasn't that he was trusted, and it would make his skin crawl if he were, but Subaru made a plan to overcome this difficult situation based on Elsa's words.
In the process of fleeing, they had already flowed quite far south. From here, it wouldn't take long to move to the place Elsa indicated.
The people of the slums, whom Subaru had bribed with the holy gold coins Elsa possessed, were obstructing the guards' search and guiding them the wrong way. There was no problem.

"What I don't understand is your goal."

"I want to make you owe me one. It might come in handy someday."

"Owe you one, huh. That's strange. —You want to kill me so badly."

While lending Elsa a shoulder and helping her escape, she said that. Elsa's black eyes looked up at Subaru from close range, trying to peer into the emotions within.
But there was no need to be suspicious and peer inside. The answer in Subaru's black eyes was exactly what Elsa had picked up.
Subaru wanted to kill Elsa right now if possible. However, if he challenged her without thinking of the consequences, Subaru wouldn't win even against a dying Elsa, and in the first place, that was short-sighted.

Subaru currently assumed that the situation he was in was a very biased game board.
A battle based on the premise of considering every move, trial and error, and making the best move.
If he thought of this as something like shogi, it wasn't as simple as winning by just driving away the pieces on the board one by one.
If possible, he would swap enemies and allies. If there was an opening, he wouldn't hesitate to change his standing.

For that purpose, he should even utilize the opponent he deeply hated and wanted to kill.

"I want to kill you. Surely, I'll kill you someday. But not now."

"I see."

Subaru revealed his true intentions to Elsa, as there was no need to hide them.
If Elsa were a cautious person, she would have killed Subaru on the spot to eliminate any future trouble. But Subaru was certain that wouldn't happen.

It was a certainty of killing intent that could only be held by Subaru, who had been killed by Elsa eighty-eight times—over eighty of those times.

"Wonderful. To bind you and me together with hatred. Eventually, surely. Your words will surely be proven to me. That is very, very wonderful."

"————"

Loosening her blood-stained red lips, Elsa smiled like a maiden in love.
Seeing that smile up close, Subaru thought from the bottom of his heart that this woman was hideous.

"We'll surely meet again, won't we?"

"Bye-bye, big brother. I'll be grateful for Elsa's sake."

Handing Elsa over to the girl with dark blue hair who had joined them, Subaru breathed a sigh of relief.
Waiting in the abandoned house Elsa had indicated was a girl still in her early teens. At first, Subaru was surprised by her youth, but the girl handled Elsa's wounds with a practiced attitude and quickly finished preparations to flee from the Royal Capital, leaving the abandoned house early.

Securing a means of contact with Elsa was the reward Subaru had obtained.
Whether it was a reward commensurate with the danger he had faced was uncertain, but—

"That depends on my actions from here on, I guess."

Taking off his tracksuit jacket, which was stained with dried blood, Subaru tied it around his waist and started walking.
His feet were headed toward the opposite side of the slums, in the direction of the loot cellar. He was only concerned about whether Satella had safely retrieved her insignia at that place where Elsa could not go.

"————"

Taking his time to reach the loot cellar, Subaru widened his eyes at the sight.

"This was unexpected."

The sight spreading in front of Subaru as he muttered that was the loot cellar frozen in ice.
Rather than being frozen, it was more accurate to say that the loot cellar was enclosed in ice. Wondering what had happened, he did a light inquiry with a nearby resident.

"The old man who was the boss of the loot cellar and his granddaughter were hauled off by the guards. Apparently, they made an enemy of a scary magic user or something... I don't want to get involved."

"Are the granddaughter and the old man safe? What about the magic user?"

"I heard they weren't hurt, but I didn't see either of them clearly. That's enough, right?"

Perhaps finding Subaru's intense momentum eerie, the man shook off Subaru's arm and hurried away into the darkness of the back alley.
Watching his back and ruminating on the man's words, Subaru breathed a sigh of relief.

He didn't know how much of the man's words were true, but at least he shouldn't have misheard a story about someone dying for what he just heard.
It was unavoidable that Felt and Old Man Rom were caught by the guards, given the business they were in. They should reflect on their actions while eating prison food for a while.

And knowing that Satella was safely saved as Subaru had hoped—

"Well then. From here, what should I do?"

Scratching his head, Subaru realized that his goal had become empty.
Summoned to another world, given a power like "Return by Death," utilizing it to save that meddlesome and cute silver-haired half-elf.
To achieve that, he had died eighty-seven times and reached this point.

"I messed up. Should I have died and redone it to find out why Elsa was trying to steal the insignia from Satella...?"

He should have asked while carrying the injured Elsa, but he had forgotten.
However, if he had pried into Elsa's circumstances there and she found out that Subaru was emotionally on Satella's side, he didn't know how Elsa would have acted.
As a result, both Subaru and Satella were alive now. If that was the correct answer—

"If possible, I want to know more about Satella, but..."

He didn't know where Satella came from, where she went, or if they would meet again.
There was a way to repeat until he found out.
If there was another way—

"—I wonder if you guys will tell me?"

In front of the frozen loot cellar, Subaru turned around while putting both hands in his pockets.
There was no sign of people. And yet, in Subaru's vision, he could see countless shadows of people standing.

Mediocre faces, men and women of all ages moderately mixed, an uncoordinated group.
If there was only one thing unified within that group, it was their eyes.

In all of them, their eyes were dead. Dyed in madness, seeking ecstasy, for that purpose.
Surely, if he looked in a mirror, Subaru would have the same eyes.

"————"

Loosening his cheeks at that sentiment, Subaru looked up at the sky.
A moon so clear it was scary looked down silently at the frozen loot cellar and the madmen.

It was nearly two months later that Satella's identity was revealed in detail.

"Royal Selection! Silver-haired half-elf! Recommended by Margrave Roswaal L. Mathers! Not Satella, but Emilia—!"

Having had no progress and with detailed information that had been cut off suddenly jumping in, Subaru clapped his hands with an expression of joy.

The information unexpectedly flowed in through the announcement of the "Royal Selection" which was posted not only in the Royal Capital but throughout the country following an order from the Royal Castle.
At first, Subaru, who had made light of it as just an election for a single country, widened his eyes at the information expressed primitively through signboards set up in the city.

Because among the five women set up as candidates was the girl he had been searching for.

"Royal Selection, Royal Selection, huh... A king candidate means a noble status. No wonder she was overflowing with elegance just by walking. Emilia, Emilia, I see."

Now that her real name was known, Subaru's heart became light as if it had grown wings.

"————"

"—The man who is killed by you."

Feeling the impact pierce the center of his chest, Subaru smiled thinly.

Collapsing on the spot from his knees, Subaru fell without support and rolled.
Without reaching Emilia, he rolled unsightly and helplessly on the stone pavement.

Eventually, if he lay flat on his back and looked up at the sky, there was a blue sky looking down on the red Royal Capital.
In a world sandwiched between red and blue, Natsuki Subaru met his end.

"Why?"

Subaru, who closed his eyes and was about to accept the end, noticed something and opened his eyelids.
Standing right next to the fallen Subaru was Emilia, looking down. The tears that fell from her eyes hit Subaru's cheek and made him open his eyes.

"Why?"

The question was repeated and layered.
Subaru thought about what that 'why' meant.

'Why' did he do such a thing?
'Why' did it have to end up like this?
'Why' did he come here to be killed by her?

Surely, there were various 'whys' there.
He wanted to give an answer to all those 'whys,' but the time left for Subaru was only a very small amount.

So Subaru, as if letting out a breath, put the answer in his final breath.

"—I love you."

※ ※ ※ ※ ※ ※ ※ ※ ※ ※ ※ ※ ※

'Death' was approaching. The familiar 'death.'

Every time he met 'death,' Subaru was taken to a dark and lonely place he didn't know.
It was truly, truly lonely, a place he couldn't endure if he were alone.

Every time he met 'death,' he was sent out from here.
To that world of blood and pain, tears and wailing, and where there was something he loved just a little bit.

But it was fine now.
He was already satisfied.

"————"

He felt like someone was whispering something in this dim world, welcoming Subaru.

It was a comfort, it was an encouragement, it was a confirmation, and it was a confession.

Why was it? This was not the person he loved.
The person he loved would fulfill her wish and be rewarded in a place that was not here, in a place where he was not.

Because he paid many sacrifices for that, and finally offered himself.
So it was fine. Even if she didn't save him. Salvation was given at the very beginning.

"————"

It was surely a call.
Gently, affectionately, lovingly, his name was called.

So Subaru, while accepting that his existence was leaving the spiral of 'death' and moving away.
While accepting it, he responded to the words of '————'.

"————"

—Even if you reject me, I will not forget you.
