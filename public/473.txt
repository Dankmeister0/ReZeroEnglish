"—Hey, do you know my name?"

At that question, the girl, Amyu Sears, caught her breath slightly.

It was an ordinary, trivial question.
It should have been a boring question that required neither much time nor resolve to answer.
If you know it, you know it. If you don't, you don't. That should have been all.

"————"

However, at a question that seemed so simple on paper, Amyu could not find her voice.

Life is a series of choices.
That was a truth that even Amyu, who had only just turned fourteen, had learned well enough through the experiences of her short life.

In life, one must make decisions about everything.
It starts with the trivialities of daily life and ranges to major decisions that sway the course of one's existence. But regardless of the scale, life is made entirely of decisions.

And now, the question posed to Amyu Sears carried the greatest weight of her fourteen years, and perhaps represented the biggest decision of her life.
And yet, it was that very same boring, unspecial question.

"—Hey, do you know my name?"

The repeated question tightened around Amyu's throat.
However, the person who posed the question seemingly had no such intention. Repeating the question once more was an act of kindness; the sense of consideration felt strangely unbalanced.

If the one tormenting Amyu was the questioner, the one most concerned for Amyu was also the questioner.
She understood that all he truly sought was the answer to the question.
That was why Amyu had no choice but to grope for the correct option without any clues, finding it through a dialogue with her inner self.

—What, exactly, was the correct answer?

Did knowing it or not knowing it better suit the questioner's expectations?
Or, even if she didn't know, would it be better to say she did? Even if she did know, would it be better to say she didn't? —Amyu's heart screamed at the restricted choice.

"—Hey, do you... know... my... name?"

As if growing impatient, the phrasing of the repeated question was broken down into finer segments.
Perhaps he was worried that his words weren't getting through; that anxiety clawed at Amyu's chest. Honestly, she was certain that failing to say anything, failing to produce an answer, and failing to make a decision was the one thing that would absolutely never suit the questioner's intent.

She couldn't end this without saying anything, without giving an answer, without making a decision.
She wouldn't be released. —That much was certain.

"————"

Unable to speak, Amyu stared back into the black pupils directly in front of her and pondered.
Inside those black pupils that looked empty, she saw her own haggard, terrible face.

Though she didn't go out of her way to say it, she had been aware that her appearance was decent enough to be looked at, but now there was no trace of that; she was simply cowering, terrified by the dominant power of the questioner before her.
As if she had aged decades in an instant, Amyu's face was exhausted.
She felt so weak, as if she might die from the pressure of the question alone—,

"—Hey, do you know my name?"

Suddenly, the pressure that suggested death instead gave Amyu a sense of hope.
While experiencing the sensation of her chest being crushed by breathlessness, a premonition took hold of her: that by answering the question, she might be released from it.
The moment she thought that, she felt the heavy sensation dominating her entire body begin to fade.

The breathlessness came from the pressure of suspicion and doubt over what she should answer. Because she wanted to be released from that, she suppressed her anxiety regarding the initial question.
It might have been a kind of self-preservation instinct, but to Amyu in this moment, it felt like a divine revelation. Therefore, her lips trembled, and she stared back into the black pupils once more.

"—Hey, do you know my name?"

By answering the question, she would be released from this moment's breathlessness.
With that single-minded thought, Amyu finally moved her twitching tongue and spun the words.

"N-No..."

She didn't know; Amyu followed her heart and answered so.
Moving her thoughts away from the choices that had been spinning endlessly in her head, she answered the question with a pure state of mind.

In fact, she knew neither the name nor the face of the questioner before her.
Living in a remote backwater, a place that should be called the frontier, even the great events of the kingdom she heard rumors of felt like occurrences in a far-off land.
So, no matter how great a person he might be, to her, he was a stranger—,

"—I see."

It was a short sentence.
She couldn't distinguish whether the emotion contained in those words was deep feeling or disappointment.
However, Amyu's decision had been made. The rest was a matter for the questioner.

If life is a series of choices and decisions, then it is the same for everyone.
Just as Amyu had chosen, the questioner would also choose.

"————"

Quietly, the fourteen-year-old girl waited for the questioner's choice.

—Alone in the middle of a village where no one but herself remained, she waited for the answer.

"Hey, Subaru. Isn't it about time for lunch?"

Stopping at that call, Natsuki Subaru rubbed his stomach.
The time was already past noon, and now that it was mentioned, he did feel a fair amount of hunger. The reason he hadn't noticed until it was pointed out was that he had been focused on walking with a blank mind.
The highway was so long, continuing forever, that he had to concentrate just on walking. Thinking about the road ahead, it was a dauntingly long journey that made his heart feel heavy.

"Though, rest is important. Sorry, Emilia-tan. Did I make you tired?"

"No, I'm perfectly fine. But I was just worried about you, Subaru, since you've been walking the whole time. If you're okay, then it's fine..."

"No, no, I was just getting hungry myself. My stomach was about to stick to my back. Phew, that was close."

"Really? That sounds like a ve—ry dangerous situation..."

Hearing Subaru's answer as he exaggeratedly held his stomach, the silver-haired girl—Emilia—put her hand to her mouth and giggled.
With a voice like a silver bell, the most lovely-faced girl in the world smiled. Seeing that smile up close, Subaru scratched his head and let his own lips break into a grin.
Then, from the side of the two—,

"I suppose you shouldn't tease Emilia so much. No matter how hungry you are, it's impossible for your stomach to stick to your back, in fact."

"Oops."

Turning toward the voice, there stood Beatrice with her short arms crossed and a composed look on her face. At the critique from the girl in the flamboyant dress, Subaru narrowed his eyes slightly.
Seeing that, Beatrice sharpened her well-shaped eyebrows, saying, "What is it, I wonder?"

"That look on your face, Subaru, it's bothering me for some reason. What's wrong, I suppose?"

"I just thought it was cute how you said you were hungry. Say it one more time."

"Muki—! Don't get hung up on weird things, in fact!"

Blushing, Beatrice snapped at Subaru's pointless banter. Seeing Beatrice like that, Subaru and Emilia looked at each other and laughed.
Seeing them, Beatrice puffed out her cheeks even more, looking adorable. —This sensation was certainly unmistakable. And she would surely be displeased if he told her that directly.
After confirming that, Subaru rolled up his sleeves with a "Right," and—

"Well then, I'll take Emilia-tan up on her kindness and make it mealtime. I'll show off my skills today. Any requests?"

"Ah, then, I'd like you to use mayonnaise. Look, Ram just finished remaking some mayonnaise for us, right?"

"Yes, yes, for Emilia-tan's order, it would be my pleasure! Fortunately, thanks to Ram, we have plenty of stock. That girl finally realized the charm of mayonnaise..."

"—Stop saying such foolish things."

"Whoa!?"

Subaru, who had put down his luggage and started setting up camp off the side of the highway, was looked down upon by a pair of light pink eyes as a set of slender legs suddenly stood beside him.
A valiant figure with arms crossed. It was a girl wearing a maid uniform, yet possessing a gaze entirely unrelated to the spirit of hospitality—,

"That's quite a greeting, Ram. At least let me finish what I was saying."

"Hah! Stop making me laugh. The only reason Ram lends a hand in making mayonnaise is because it's a business order. Ram, opening her heart to a condiment that is merely white and sour? Horrifying."

"Horrifying is going too far! Besides, even you can't deny the destructive power of using mayonnaise on steamed potatoes! I won't let you call that taste a lie!"

The flavor of slathering plenty of mayonnaise onto hot, salty steamed potatoes.
The sweet and forbidden taste that dominates the tongue is a paradise that anyone who enjoys mayonnaise should experience at least once—there is an ultimate, inseparable compatibility between potatoes and mayonnaise.
Recalling that magnificent harmony, that symphony of flavor, Subaru's throat tightened. Right next to him, he saw Emilia and Beatrice's eyes sparkle, as if they were imagining the same taste.

"Look at the reaction of those two cute gluttons! Can you still call mayonnaise a heresy after seeing this!?"

"...I see. It's as Barusu says. The greatness of steamed potatoes is such that it even gives value to a dubious condiment like mayonnaise."

"It's not that, it's the other way around! No, it's not even about being the other way around, you're stubborn, Nee-sama!"

Ram would not budge, insisting that the contribution of the steamed potato was the greater one.
Subaru's fist trembled at her stubbornness, but Emilia stepped between them with a wry smile and a "Now, now."

"Calm down, Subaru. I like steamed potatoes, and I like mayonnaise, and I like dishes with mayonnaise on steamed potatoes. Besides, Ram is just being a little bit dishonest."

"...Emilia-sama, please stop acting as if you can speak for Ram's thoughts as you please. The way you put it makes Ram's target age seem lower."

"Eh, I'm sorry. But, target age...?"

Emilia was bewildered by Ram's irrational and cryptic phrasing.
However, what permeated their exchange was not hostility, but an undeniable sense of trust and affection.
With this, Emilia and Ram had built a surprisingly ideal relationship. It was a bit problematic that Ram had too little awareness of being a servant to call it a master-servant relationship, but there was a bond between them that transcended that.
Subaru, more than anyone, understood that well.
At any rate—,

"It's too bad for Nee-sama, but Emilia-tan's words take priority over everything. That's the style of this party. So, today's lunch is decided: all mayonnaise!"

"Yay, hooray!"

As Emilia clapped her hands happily, Ram let out a weary sigh. Still, the fact that she didn't press the matter further showed that she was plenty soft on Emilia.
Of course, since Subaru was a born mayo-lover, this menu was a result of favoritism.

"Of course, Beako loves mayonnaise just like I do. You're happy, right?"

"I won't deny it, in fact. But it's irritating when you say it like you've seen through me, I suppose. Betty's anger won't be cleared so easily, in fact. In exchange, I desire a delicious meal, I suppose."

"Hehe—, as you wish—."

Subaru bowed to Beatrice, who puffed out her flat chest and made her cute order. After confirming that Emilia and the others looked satisfied with his theatrical attitude, he set about preparing the meal.
That said, he couldn't do anything too elaborate for an outdoor meal. Of course, if he used the power of magic, he could utilize fire and water to some extent, but—

"I'm sorry, Subaru. I can't really help you."

"I told you, there's no need to apologize. This is exactly where I get to show off my ingenuity, right? On the contrary, you're free to fall in love with me all over again for my unexpected creativity."

"Geez, you're such a dummy."

Smiling back at the faintly smiling Emilia, Subaru put effort into the meal with the ingredients on hand.
Food is the vitality of daily life. He didn't want to cut corners here. Checking the contents of the luggage, Subaru labored to prepare a meal that would meet everyone's requests as much as possible.

The members accompanying him were all people who looked like they enjoyed their food.
He hadn't thought about it much before, but it's a joyful thing to have the food you've made eaten with such relish. It also leads to motivation for the one cooking.

If it was such a simple thing, he should have properly conveyed his joy to his mother.

"Hum, hum, hum..."

Keeping such sentiments in a corner of his mind, Subaru's cheeks softened at the sight of Emilia and the others looking curiously and happily at his hands as he cooked. He could even hear humming. She was tone-deaf.

"Oh my, today's cook is Big Brother. I'm a little worried."

As he was preparing the meal, Meili peeked her head out. She toyed with her dark blue braid with her finger, directing a gaze at Subaru that was far more alluring than her age would suggest. Subaru looked back at her with a suspicious expression.

"What do you mean, worried? I won't let such a cheeky girl eat."

"Oh no, that's abuse. Besides, Big Brother, you try to put that mayonnaise condiment on everything, don't you? I don't really like that stuff very much."

"W-What did you say... Even you don't understand the charm of mayonnaise...!?"

"Don't make a face like it's the end of the world."

Meili sighed in exasperation at Subaru's stunned face. However, the shock Subaru received from her response was immeasurable.
If not the end of the world, he felt a shock equivalent to the end of the year.

"Still, you are taking it a bit too dramatically. Miss Meili is simply saying she cannot keep pace with your attitude of trying to pair mayonnaise with every ingredient."

"Mm..."

Julius, the handsome man whose very walk seemed elegant, followed up on Meili's statement as she showed her rejection of mayonnaise.
Julius, approaching them, gave Subaru a faint smile and—

"If I may add, I am of the same opinion as Miss Meili. Mayonnaise itself is not so bad, but if it is paired with everything, the initial impression fades. Please exercise self-restraint."

"You're a nitpicking, noisy guy. This is a problem between me and Meili, so you don't need to butt in."

"Maintaining a healthy environment for the group is also a role of a member. If the seeds of discord are overlooked, they may bud and bloom into something regrettable. I consider it my duty to offer advice before that happens, do you understand?"

"You always have a comeback for everything, don't you!"

Julius poked at Subaru's lack of foresight with his usual roundabout phrasing. Becoming irritated by his words and actions, Subaru raised his voice.
Then, Emilia, who had been listening to their exchange, burst out laughing with a "Fufu," and—

"Emilia-tan?"

"As always, Subaru and Julius are such go—od friends."

At Emilia's gaze, which looked upon them as something heartwarming, Subaru's shoulders slumped in exhaustion. Julius, who was in the same position, also closed one eye while touching his bangs.
Only at this timing did Subaru and Julius's opinions seem to coincide, but there seemed to be a vast gap between their opinion and Emilia's perception.
In her jewel-like dark blue eyes, the exchange between Subaru and Julius just now appeared to be a scene of close friendship.

"Listen, Emilia-tan. I say this all the time, but that's..."

"That's?"

That is, no matter how you look at it, a great misunderstanding.
Subaru tried to explain to the tilting Emilia—

"That's..."

"————"

The words of explanation he tried to continue suddenly stuck, and Subaru's tongue stiffened.
Immediately, the movement of Emilia, who had been smiling and tilting her head before him, froze. —No, it wasn't just Emilia.
Around Subaru as he cooked, the people who had been talking peacefully until just now stopped moving exactly as they were, everyone becoming completely rigid.

—As if the world had stopped time, waiting for Subaru's next reaction.

However, the sound of the wind, the scent of the trees, and the boiling water told him that wasn't the case.
The world had not stopped. —Only Emilia and the others had stopped.

As if waiting for Subaru's image, which tried to reproduce them, to catch up.
Only Emilia and the others had stopped—

"—Master~! I took a quick look ahead!"

"————"

Suddenly, an anomaly broke into that frozen world of silence.
A figure that had leaped vigorously jumped in front of Subaru, who was sitting on a large rock and tending the fire.

"Shaula has returned with a flourish! Master, please praise me, hug me, and love me!"

The one who said that with a full smile was a tall, beautiful woman.
She was a person with long brown hair tied in a single ponytail, boldly exposing her healthy white skin. Showing off her feminine, curvaceous body without hesitation, she—Shaula—wore a carefree smile.

"————"

However, Subaru's reaction to Shaula's full-on display of affection was not favorable. That was only natural; the place she had jumped into was the very spot where Emilia had been smiling just a moment ago.
Her noisy, grand leap had scattered Emilia and blown away the other frozen figures as well. Subaru frowned at that fact.

"A-Are? What's wrong, Master? Did I do something again?"

"...No, it's nothing. Don't worry about it. It's not like it's your fault."

"Is that so? Then I won't worry! Ah, more importantly, Master, I'm so happy you're preparing food! I was starving~"

Shaula's face clouded for a moment at Subaru's expression, but she immediately took his excuse at face value and, with a face that had forgotten her previous anxiety, clung to Subaru's arm.
Feeling the intense softness of Shaula's body pressed against his elbow and shoulder, Subaru curtly brushed her off, saying, "Hey, let go."

"Ahn! Master, you're so mean... but I won't give up. I'll keep attacking like this and aim for Master's heart, ulterior motives, and manliness."

"It feels like all of those are on the same target line... Also, don't grab the arm of someone who's cooking. What if I cut myself or get burned?"

"If that happens, I'll gently lick the affected area for you! I'll lick and lick and never let go, twenty-four-seven!"

"That'll just make it soggy..."

Shaula asserted herself with both hands raised, acting more with momentum than anything else. Subaru's shoulders slumped at Shaula's attitude, and he laughed weakly.
Seeing that, Shaula raised her eyebrows with a "Yaya!"

"Did Master just laugh? Did something funny happen?"

"The funny thing was you, you."

"Eh, is that... a proposal...?"

"No, it's not!?"

He didn't know how the conversation had leaped so far, but Subaru delivered a flick to the forehead of the blushing, fidgeting Shaula, conveying both protest and denial.
Receiving it, Shaula groaned, "Auu~," and—

"But, but, 'You're an interesting woman' is a classic exchange for a man and woman falling in love! The words that follow should only be 'Become my woman'!?"

"Where exactly does your biased knowledge come from? That's a pattern so worn out you can hardly find it even in my hometown anymore..."

In shoujo manga or games for women, male characters who used such first-meeting events might not have been rare, but Subaru didn't know if that still worked today.
At any rate, the reason he found Shaula amusing wasn't to woo her.
It was just that Shaula's reaction, to Subaru—

"—What you say is unpredictable and fresh."

"————"

"So, talking to you is... not bad. That's for sure."

He wouldn't go so far as to say that talking to anyone other than Shaula was painful.
However, when he talked to anyone other than Shaula, Subaru naturally became aware of his own inadequacies, and that was painful.
It wasn't anyone's fault. —The one at fault was always himself.

"The one at fault is always me. So..."

"In other words, this time it really is a proposal!?"

"I said it's not!"

The atmosphere for a serious conversation didn't even last a second, and Shaula exploded with dissatisfaction at the shouting Subaru. She flailed her arms and legs, saying "Ehh~," and—

"But, but, the development where someone blows a fresh breeze into a boring and gray life is the classic of classics, isn't it~"

"No, like I said, where is your fountain of biased knowledge springing from..."

Subaru gave up, as his attempt at a serious talk was completely derailed by Shaula.
With a faint softening of his cheeks, Subaru concentrated on cooking.

At Subaru's hands as he cooked by the side of the highway, the meal prepared was for two.
For Subaru and Shaula—there was no portion for Emilia, Beatrice, Ram, Meili, or Julius, with whom he should have been enjoying conversation until just now.

—Re-recognizing that fact, Subaru's chest felt terribly empty and cried out in pain.

Being reminded time and again that he was an incomplete existence wore down his nerves.
It was obvious that even just imagining it wasn't a healthy mentality, but having actually been placed in that environment, he could vividly feel the sensation of his soul being worn away.

To put it simply, Natsuki Subaru's current state of mind was exactly like that.


"—Master, Master, where are we going next?"

"Let's see..."

Shaula walked beside Subaru, humming happily.
Raising his gaze at her question, Subaru thought while looking down the highway.

"For now, it would be best to head west. You're aiming for a human settlement, right? If you want to get away from people, you should turn around and plunge into the Great Cascade."

"Nee-sama makes some incredible suggestions... I'll just take the sentiment."

It was a very Ram-like way of speaking, mixing poison with honest advice. Keeping that in mind, Subaru followed the suggestion and decided on a policy of heading west.

"I plan to keep heading west. ...But what about you?"

"Eh? Well, I'm sticking right by Master, of course! We were separated for so long, I can't bear to be away from Master for even a second. Please take care of the Shaula who will watch over your life from 'Good Morning' to 'Good Morning'."

"From 'Good Morning' to 'Good Morning'... That's scary! That's all day!"

"Every day and every night, too."

While trembling at the rather intense stalking notice, Subaru looked down with a troubled face.
Honestly, the situation of walking together with Shaula felt strange.

Subaru, who harbored dark and gloomy guilt, and Shaula, who acted wildly and openly. As a combination, they might be balanced in terms of positive and negative tension, but while Subaru was saved in no small part by Shaula's words and actions, he couldn't see any merit for Shaula in being with him.

"The fact that Master is Master is the most important thing to me."

"...Honestly, I don't really get it. Are you seriously going to follow me forever?"

"Of course! Until death do us part! The love nurtured by the time we couldn't meet won't wither from something like this~. I'll never leave Master's side no matter what. I'll even give birth to enough children for a baseball team."

"You're throwing some serious fastballs, aren't you..."

Puffing out her ample chest, Shaula looked at him with sparkling eyes. Blocking that gaze with his palm, Subaru's heart as he sighed was complex and mysterious.

Shaula's direct words and actions—he wasn't sure if he should call them a courtship, but Subaru didn't exactly feel unhappy about them as a man.
Setting aside her eccentric behavior, Shaula was beautiful and charming.

"————"

However, Natsuki Subaru's heart was not swayed by Shaula's temptation.
Because such human emotions were only permitted for proper humans. In short, they were a qualification that the current him could never reach.

"I think you should be a little kinder to Shaula, Subaru. It's pitiful when she's trying so hard."

In response to Subaru's hesitation, a silver-bell voice suddenly cut in. Turning his gaze slightly to the side, he saw Emilia peeking at his face.
At her cute, pouting expression, Subaru closed one eye.

"...Emilia-tan, you say that, but that's a pretty cruel thing, you know? Even though you know how I felt about you."

"Mm... yeah, maybe. Maybe so. I'm sorry. Of course, I'm lonely too, but I think it's no good to mope forever. Because..."

"Because?"

"Because, I'm already—"

Narrowing her dark blue eyes, Emilia tried to continue her words. Instinctively, Subaru wanted to cut her off, not wanting to hear it, but that was a difficult thing to do.
Not listening to Emilia's words was harder than interrupting the words of a god.
However—,

"Oops."

Even if it was impossible to interrupt voluntarily, being interrupted by an external factor was permitted.
Suddenly, hearing a noise approaching from behind, Subaru shifted his consciousness away from Emilia and gave way to the side of the highway. The sound of approaching wheels announced the approach of a running dragon carriage.
Quickly pulling Shaula's arm, the two of them escaped from the dragon carriage's path.

"Yan, Master, you're so forceful..."

"Get off me."

Taking advantage of being pulled by the arm, Shaula clung to him, but Subaru elegantly evaded her. The avoided Shaula crashed into a tree along the highway and let out a cry of "Gyafun!"
Ignoring Shaula, who was now rubbing her reddened nose, Subaru waited for the dragon carriage to pass and tried to return to the highway—,

"Hm?"

Ahead of his gaze, the dragon carriage that had overtaken them came to a noisy stop.
It didn't look like a mechanical failure. In that case, the only reason for the stop he could think of was Subaru and Shaula.
It was still high noon, but it was a deserted road. Subaru momentarily heightened his vigilance, wondering if they had encountered bandits.

"—By any chance, are you Mr. Natsuki Subaru?"

However, that vigilance quickly dissipated at the call from the person who got down from the driver's seat of the dragon carriage.
Called by name, Subaru relaxed his guard as a tall young man, who should have been sitting in the driver's seat, approached him.

He was a person of about the same age as Subaru, with gray hair tied back.
His gentle face made him look slender, but his build was surprisingly solid. He didn't have the air of someone engaged in manual labor, but perhaps his work wasn't entirely unrelated to such things.
Setting aside those first impressions, what was important to Subaru was that this young man recognized him and called him by name.

"Ah, I knew it, it is you, Mr. Natsuki. It's been a long time."

"Ah—, er..."

The young man approached with an air of being an acquaintance, despite the slight distance. Hesitating on how to respond, Subaru looked around.
Receiving that gaze, Emilia, Beatrice, and the others shook their heads, indicating to Subaru that they had no idea. If Emilia and the others didn't know, Subaru was at a loss, but he hesitated to start a conversation as if he knew the person.
That was Subaru's specialty, but for various reasons, he didn't want to do that now.
Seeing Subaru hesitating in such a state of mind, the young man frowned his well-shaped eyebrows and—

"Oh, have you perhaps forgotten? It's me, Regin Suwen... My brother, Otto Suwen, has been in your care. We met once before in the village."

"Regin... Oh, Otto's brother!"

"Calling him 'that Otto' makes it sound like it's someone else's business."

Laughing at the unnatural phrasing, Subaru clapped his hands in front of the young man—Regin—as he finally understood. Unfortunately, he didn't remember Regin's appearance, but he knew Otto.
Now that it was mentioned, Regin and Otto's faces shared many similar features. It was certain that they were brothers and that he was an acquaintance.

"Sorry, sorry. My head is a bit full of various things right now. I couldn't come up with your name right away, or something like that."

"No, you must be in a busy position, so it can't be helped. News of the Royal Selection has reached even my ears in the countryside. ...Is my brother causing you any trouble?"

"It's not like Otto-kun is a bother at all. All of us are helped in so many ways thanks to Otto-kun."

"That's right. Otto has been a great help to the entire camp... or something like that. The reality is that things would be much more unstable if he weren't there."

"I see. If that's the case, then I'm glad..."

Regin, who had been somewhat apologetic, seemed slightly relieved to hear about his brother's current situation. Then, he looked back and forth between Subaru and Shaula, who was standing a little distance away.

"Even so, what are you doing in a place like this, Mr. Natsuki? Walking along the highway without even using a dragon carriage must be quite exhausting..."

"Ah—, that's a very reasonable opinion, and I'd use a dragon carriage if I could, but it's difficult due to various circumstances. Also, as for what I'm doing..."

Subaru cut his words there and thought for a moment.
Then, finding the appropriate words—

"Right now, I'm in the middle of re-evaluating myself... a 'journey to find myself', I guess."

"A journey to find yourself?"

"Yeah, that's right. A journey to find myself... ku, hahaha."

His own words were so funny that Subaru couldn't help but laugh.
Finding oneself is a phrase associated with youth, but could there be any other words so appropriate for the current Subaru? Thinking that, he couldn't help but find it hilarious.
Regin seemed to sense something was off about Subaru's behavior and showed concern, asking, "Are you alright?"

"As I thought, is there something wrong with your health? The reason I was a moment late in recognizing you was exactly that; you look quite different from before..."

"My health isn't bad, really. If we're talking about my mood, I'm one step away from the worst. But I was able to hold on thanks to you, Regin. I'm relieved to have met you here."

Interrupting Regin's words, Subaru bowed deeply to express his gratitude. Seeing Subaru's posture, Regin's confusion only deepened.
