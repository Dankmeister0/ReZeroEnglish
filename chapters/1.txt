Arc 1 Prologue: "The Afterglow of the Beginning"

—This is seriously bad.

Feeling the sensation of the hard ground against his face, he realized he had fallen face down.
He couldn't put any strength into his body, and the feeling in his fingertips was already gone.
However, a heat so intense it made him want to claw at his throat dominated the center of his body.


—Hot, hot, hot, hot, hot, hot, hot.


The moment he opened his mouth to let out a scream, what spilled out was not a shriek, but a clump of blood.
He coughed, vomiting out the source of his life as it welled up from his throat. Gurgle, gurgle—he threw up so much blood that bubbles of gore formed at the corners of his mouth. In his blurred vision, he could see the ground dyed bright red.


—Ah, so all of this is my blood.


There was enough blood to soak his fallen body. He'd heard that a human's blood volume is about 8% of their total weight, and losing a third of that is life-threatening—but at this point, wasn't it all out already?
The vomiting had stopped, but the cause of the "heat" that seemed to be incinerating his body was still active. His hand, which barely moved, went toward his abdomen; he felt an impossible sensation there and finally understood.


—What, my belly's been ripped open?


No wonder it felt hot. It seemed he was hallucinating "pain" as "heat."
The sharp laceration had passed almost completely through his torso, leaving him connected only by a single layer of skin at his waist.
In short, it seemed he was facing the "checkmate" of his life.

The moment he understood this, his consciousness rapidly began to fade.
Even the "heat" that had forced him to writhe until a moment ago vanished somewhere; the unpleasant sensation of blood and the feeling of his hand touching his internal organs were all taken along as companions to his departing consciousness.

The only thing left behind was his physical body, which had been refused the companionship of his "soul."
With one final effort from his vanishing consciousness, he moved that body just a little. He turned his neck upward.

Before his eyes, on the floor carpeted with fresh blood, black shoes stepped down, creating ripples.
Someone was there. And that someone was probably the one who had killed him.

Strangely, he didn't feel like trying to get a look at the opponent's face.
He didn't remember being such an opportunist that he would remain a bystander even toward someone who killed him, but his heart didn't hold a shred of interest in that person's identity.

He only wished—for her to be safe.

"—baru?"

He felt as though he heard a voice like a tinkling bell.
Since he no longer knew where his ears or nose were, it was more likely a hallucination.
Despite that, even if it was a reproduction based on memory, that voice was terribly pleasant and stirred his emotions.

That was why—


"—!"

A short scream rang out, and the carpet of blood welcomed a new participant.
The fallen body was right beside him, and there, his own arm lay stretched out sloppily.
That limp, white hand intertwined with his own blood-smeared hand.

It must have all been a coincidence.

He felt as though his faintly moving fingertips squeezed her hand back.

"...stay back."

He grabbed his departing consciousness by the scruff of its neck, forcing it to turn back to buy time.
The "pain" and the "heat" were all distant now; this was merely a loser's howl of useless struggle.


But even so—


"I will, definitely—"


"—save you."



In the next instant, he—Natsuki Subaru—lost his life.
